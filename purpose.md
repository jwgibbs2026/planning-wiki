# Purpose

## What this wiki is for

A single, publicly accessible knowledge base for understanding planning and nature in Gloucestershire — and how they connect. The audience is anyone who wants to get involved: individuals, community groups, parish councillors, ecologists, and developers.

## Goals

1. **Explain** how the planning system works in plain English — no jargon barriers
2. **Connect** related topics visually (via Obsidian graph view) so people can follow how policies, legislation, organisations, and habitats relate to each other
3. **Empower** communities to engage with planning effectively — responding to applications, influencing local plans, developing NDPs
4. **Represent** Gloucestershire specifically — local councils, organisations, habitats, NDP status, and nature data
5. **Stay current** — as legislation, policies, and local plans evolve, the wiki should reflect the latest

## Key questions this wiki should answer

- How does the planning system work, and where can I get involved?
- What are my legal rights and what protections exist for wildlife?
- What is Biodiversity Net Gain and what does it mean for developments near me?
- How can my parish council write a Neighbourhood Development Plan that genuinely protects nature?
- What organisations exist in Gloucestershire to support nature in planning?
- Which areas and habitats in Gloucestershire are most important and why?
- What planning applications are happening near me?

## Scope

- **National**: planning system, NPPF, key legislation, BNG, LNRS, GI, SuDS, protected sites, protected species
- **Gloucestershire-specific**: all 6 district councils, GWT, GCER, NDP status across the county, Gloucestershire nature data
- **Not in scope**: detailed species biology beyond planning relevance; non-Gloucestershire local detail

## Evolving thesis

The planning system is one of the most powerful levers for nature recovery in England — but most communities don't know how to use it. This wiki aims to change that by making the connections between policy, law, ecology, and local action visible and navigable.
