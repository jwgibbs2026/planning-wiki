## Concepts — Planning System

- [[Environmental Impact Assessment]] — formal EIA process; Annex I/II development; screening opinion; Environmental Statement; EcIA as biodiversity chapter (new)
- [[Flood Risk Assessment]] — FRA and Sequential Test; flood zones 1–3b; Exception Test; climate change allowances; Gloucestershire Severn Vale context (new)

## Concepts — Legislation

- [[Protection of Badgers Act 1992]] — dedicated legislation protecting badgers and their setts; distinct from WCA 1981 and EPS framework

## Concepts — Policy

- [[NPPF]] — National Planning Policy Framework: the national planning policy document; paragraphs 190–191 and footnote 67 govern major development in AONBs
- [[Seek to Further Duty]] — duty on relevant authorities to further the purposes of protected landscape designation; in force since 26 December 2023; only 10% of planning decisions in 2024 explicitly referred to it
- [[Sandford Principle]] — when conservation and enjoyment purposes conflict in a protected landscape, conservation takes priority
- [[Major Development in AONBs]] — exceptional circumstances test under NPPF paragraphs 190–191 and footnote 67; not a simple planning balance
- [[Protected Landscapes Targets and Outcomes Framework]] — national framework for monitoring nature recovery and other outcomes in protected landscapes; first data release 2024

## Concepts — Biodiversity

- [[Biodiversity]] — overview of biodiversity and its importance
- [[Biodiversity Metric]] — the statutory calculation tool for BNG; habitat units based on area, condition, distinctiveness, strategic significance; hedgerow and watercourse accounts separate (new)
- [[Conservation Covenants]] — legally binding land agreements securing off-site BNG habitats for 30+ years; introduced by Environment Act 2021; links to Biodiversity Gain Site Register (new)
- [[Habitats Regulations Assessment]] — HRA process for plans/projects affecting SACs and SPAs; two-stage: screening then Appropriate Assessment; Gloucestershire SAC/SPA sites listed (new)
- [[Nutrient Neutrality]] — planning requirement in affected catchments (incl. Severn Estuary) to demonstrate no net increase in nutrients; blocks ~16,500 homes/year nationally; addressed by Nature Restoration Fund EDPs from 2026 (new)
- [[Ecological Impact Assessment]] — EcIA process: PEA → targeted surveys → impact assessment → mitigation; CIEEM standards; BS 42020 (new)
- [[30x30 Commitment]] — UK pledge to protect 30% of land and seas by 2030; Kunming-Montreal Global Biodiversity Framework; only ~8% of England currently qualifies; GWT target of 30,000 additional ha in Gloucestershire (new)
- [[European Protected Species]] — highest tier of legal protection; EPS status, procedural obligations, three-part test; bats, GCN, otters, and hazel dormice (updated)
- [[Species of Principal Importance]] — S41 species under NERC Act 2006; material consideration in planning; includes hazel dormice, water voles, and many bird species (updated)
- [[SSSI]] — Site of Special Scientific Interest: the most important wildlife sites; 49% in favourable condition in the CNL (above national average)
- [[Important Hedgerows]] — criteria for hedgerows protected under the Hedgerows Regulations 1997; ecological (7+ woody species, listed species) and historical (pre-1850 boundaries) criteria; 42-day removal notification process (new)
- [[Agri-environment Schemes]] — SFI, Countryside Stewardship, Landscape Recovery: the primary mechanism for delivering LNRS targets on farmland; BNG off-site habitat creation link (new)
- [[Invasive Non-Native Species]] — INNS in planning: Japanese knotweed, Himalayan balsam, giant hogweed, signal crayfish, mink; legal framework; management plan requirements; Gloucestershire context (new)
- [[Calcareous and Neutral Grassland]] — species-rich Priority Habitats; NVC survey requirements; CG2/MG5 communities; Cotswolds calcareous grassland; Severn Vale floodplain meadows; high distinctiveness in BNG metric (new)
- [[Reptiles in Planning]] — slow worm, grass snake, common lizard, adder; Schedule 5 protection; survey method (artificial refugia); soft clearance and translocation; no licence required (unlike EPS) (new)
- [[Agri-environment Schemes]] — SFI, Countryside Stewardship, Landscape Recovery: the primary mechanism for delivering LNRS targets on farmland; BNG off-site habitat creation link (new)
- [[Biodiversity Gain Plan]] — statutory pre-commencement document required to approve BNG compliance; baseline, on-site/off-site habitat, management plan, 30-year commitment; priority order for gain (new)

## Concepts — Protected Species (Detailed)

- [[Bats in Planning]] — legal status, survey triggers, mitigation hierarchy, licensing, dark corridors; most common EPS in planning
- [[Bat Surveys]] — when surveys are required, methods, seasonal timing, surveyor credentials
- [[EPS Mitigation Licence]] — the licence process, three-part test, relationship to planning permission; no equivalent exists for wild birds (updated)
- [[Dark Corridors]] — unlit landscape features for bat commuting; GI and lighting policy connections; also relevant to badgers and dormice
- [[Great Crested Newts in Planning]] — legal status, 500m survey trigger, impact framework, 2-pond rule, mitigation, licensing, DLL
- [[GCN Surveys]] — eDNA sampling, population surveys, HSI, 500m/250m zones, 4-season data currency rule
- [[District Level Licensing]] — population-scale alternative to site-by-site EPS mitigation licensing for GCN
- [[Otters in Planning]] — legal status, water proximity trigger, survey methods, mammal ledges, artificial holts, site-by-site licensing; no DLL equivalent
- [[Badgers in Planning]] — legal status (Protection of Badgers Act 1992), survey triggers, sett classification, mitigation hierarchy, artificial setts, translocation caveat (TB risk), site-by-site licensing, survey confidentiality
- [[Water Voles in Planning]] — legal status (WCA 1981, not EPS), survey triggers, habitat indicators, displacement mitigation, Class Licence CL31, mink control as compensation, post-development monitoring
- [[Water Vole Surveys]] — survey triggers, habitat assessment, field signs, CIEEM competency, combined riparian survey opportunities
- [[Wild Birds in Planning]] — legal status (WCA 1981), no development licence, nesting season avoidance (March–August), survey triggers, mitigation hierarchy, nest boxes, green roofs
- [[Barn Owls in Planning]] — S41 species; buildings and old trees as survey triggers; nest box integration; common in rural Gloucestershire conversions
- [[Swifts in Planning]] — red-listed S41 species; site-faithful nesters in older buildings; survey by dawn/dusk emergence watch; swift bricks as mitigation; no licence route (new)
- [[Barn Conversions and Wildlife]] — synthesis page for the most common rural planning scenario; bats, barn owls, swallows, swifts, reptiles; prior approval; EPS licence requirements (new)
- [[Hazel Dormice in Planning]] — EPS + S41 + Red List Vulnerable; woodland/hedgerow/scrub survey triggers; 3-year survey currency; presence assumption rule; green bridges; translocation as last resort (new)
- [[Dormouse Surveys]] — nest tubes, footprint tracking tunnels, visual hazelnut/nest searches, seasonal timing, survey currency (new)

## Concepts — Green Infrastructure

- [[SuDS]] — Sustainable Urban Drainage Systems: mandatory for all new developments requiring planning permission in England; seven national standards covering runoff destination, interception, flood protection, water quality, amenity, biodiversity, and maintenance; SuDS habitats can contribute to BNG
- [[SuDS Runoff Destination Hierarchy]] — the legally mandated priority order for surface water discharge: reuse > infiltration > surface water > sewers; cost alone cannot justify lower-priority options
- [[SuDS and Biodiversity]] — Standard 6 of the national SuDS standards; biodiversity risk and opportunity assessments; BNG integration; LNRS alignment; habitat connectivity
- [[Dark Corridors]] — unlit routes for bat commuting and badger movement; connects lighting policy, GI, and BNG
- [[Green Bridges]] — vegetated crossing structures reconnecting fragmented habitat over roads and barriers; key dormouse mitigation measure (new)
- [[Lighting and Wildlife]] — artificial light at night (ALAN): impacts on bats, otters, invertebrates, dormice, birds; mitigation hierarchy; ILP guidance; Cotswolds dark sky policy; Gloucestershire context (new)
- [[Tree Preservation Orders]] — TPOs: LPA power to protect individual trees, groups, or woodland; consent required for works; Conservation Area trees; link to ancient trees (new)

## Concepts — Protected Landscapes

- [[Cotswolds National Landscape]] — largest AONB in England (787 sq miles / 2,038 km²); designated 1966; spans 15 local authority areas; 20% BNG requirement; highest planning protection equivalent to National Parks
- [[Wye Valley National Landscape]] — designated 1971; covers west Gloucestershire and the Wye gorge; also covers Forest of Dean (Crown Forest, not a National Landscape designation) (new)
- [[Wye Valley Biosphere Reserve]] — UNESCO Biosphere Reserve in the Wye Valley/Forest of Dean area; non-statutory but reflects international importance; verify current designation status (new)
- [[Seek to Further Duty]] — s.85 CRoW Act 2000 / s.245 LURA 2023; duty on relevant authorities to further purposes of protected landscape designation; in force since 26 December 2023
- [[Sandford Principle]] — conservation purpose takes priority over enjoyment when they conflict in a protected landscape
- [[Major Development in AONBs]] — exceptional circumstances test; NPPF paragraphs 190–191 and footnote 67; not a simple planning balance
- [[Protected Landscapes Targets and Outcomes Framework]] — national monitoring framework for protected landscapes; first data release 2024; key targets include 80% SSSIs in favourable condition by 2042 and 250,000 ha wildlife-rich habitat restored/created by 2042

## Entities — Organisations

- [[Forestry Commission]] — government forestry authority; non-statutory consultee for ancient woodland planning applications; manages Forest of Dean PAWS restoration; asked to ensure publicly funded woodland creation schemes reflect CNL Board guidance (updated)
- [[NBN Atlas]] — national biodiversity data repository; first check for protected species records including birds, water voles, dormice, and EPS near development sites (updated)
- [[Environment Agency]] — undertook Natural Capital accounting exercise for CNL (2022); asked to support Cotswold Nature Recovery Plan through River Basin and Catchment Management Plans; provides Upper End Allowance climate change factors required for SuDS Standard 3 compliance (updated)
- [[Cotswolds National Landscape Board]] — statutory body (legal name: Cotswolds Conservation Board); established 2004; 37 members; one of only two Conservation Boards in England; does not own or manage CNL land; produces statutory Management Plan; key planning consultee for CNL applications (new)
- [[Historic England]] — stakeholder for heritage at risk; advises on environmental land management schemes; publishes guidance on adapting historic buildings (new)
- National Landscapes Association — published guidance on applying s.85 CRoW Act "seek to further" duty (2024); formerly National Association of AONBs (new)
- [[Defra]] — issues guidance on "seek to further" duty (2024); responsible for PLTOF; own research shows 10% BNG is minimum to ensure no net loss; joint author (with MHCLG) of national SuDS standards (updated)
- [[CIEEM]] — Chartered Institute of Ecology and Environmental Management; sets the EcIA guidelines and surveyor competency standards used across protected-species work (new)
