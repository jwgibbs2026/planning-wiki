# Natural England — Wild Birds Planning Guidance

**Source type:** Natural England standing advice (material planning consideration)
**URL:** https://www.gov.uk/guidance/wild-birds-surveys-and-mitigation-for-development-projects
**Ingested:** 2026-06-10

## What this source covers

This is Natural England's standing advice for local planning authorities assessing planning applications that may affect wild birds. It is a material planning consideration for England, meaning LPAs must take it into account when determining applications.

The guidance covers:
- The legal basis for wild bird protection under the Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981
- When surveys are required
- Avoidance, mitigation, and compensation measures
- The critical rule that **no development licence exists** for bird harm
- Biodiversity enhancement expectations

---

## Key findings

### No development licence for birds
Unlike [[European Protected Species]] (bats, great crested newts, otters), Natural England cannot issue licences permitting development harm to wild birds. Limited licences exist only for public health, air safety, scientific research, and crop protection — not construction. This makes avoidance mandatory, not optional.

### All wild birds are protected
The baseline protection under WCA 1981 is broad — it applies to all wild bird species, not just rare ones. Prohibited acts include killing, injuring, taking, nest destruction, egg taking, and possession.

### Schedule 1 birds receive enhanced protection
Species listed in Schedule 1 of WCA 1981 receive additional protection against intentional or reckless disturbance near active nests or during nest-building. Surveys for Schedule 1 species must be conducted by qualified ecologists holding relevant licences.

### Nesting season avoidance (March–August)
The general breeding season window during which vegetation clearance and intrusive works should be avoided. This is the single most practical and universally applicable rule for development sites with any vegetation.

### Survey triggers
Surveys are required when development affects: natural habitats (wetland, woodland, scrub, meadow, moorland), mature gardens, trees over 100 years old or with cavities, or buildings supporting nesting birds. Species-based triggers include barn owls, Schedule 1 birds, S41 species, and red/amber-listed species.

### Mitigation hierarchy
1. **Avoidance** — locate development away from breeding areas; time works outside March–August
2. **Displacement** — outside breeding season only: block nest access, clear vegetation, use deterrents
3. **Replacement** — nest boxes (ideally integrated into brickwork), peregrine/black redstart ledges, native planting, biodiverse green roofs, hedgerow connections

### No net loss / pre-establishment
Replacement habitat must achieve no net loss, with like-for-like or superior alternatives established **before** works begin — not delivered post-development.
