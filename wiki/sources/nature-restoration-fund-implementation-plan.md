# Nature Restoration Fund — Implementation Plan

**Source type**: Government implementation plan (MHCLG / Natural England)
**URL**: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/nature-restoration-fund-implementation-plan/implementing-the-nature-restoration-fund
**Legislation**: Planning and Infrastructure Act 2025
**Status**: Secondary legislation in development; first EDPs expected Spring–Summer 2026

## Summary

Explains how the Nature Restoration Fund (NRF) will work in practice. The NRF is an alternative mechanism for developers to meet environmental obligations — instead of site-specific mitigation, developer payments are pooled and used by Natural England for strategic conservation through Environmental Delivery Plans (EDPs).

## Core Mechanism

- **Environmental Delivery Plans (EDPs)**: Strategic conservation packages for specific areas/species
- Developer levy payments pooled; Natural England contracts conservation delivery via competitive tendering
- Upon EDP approval, participating developers exempt from individual habitat assessments or species licences
- Mostly voluntary participation

## Three Phases

1. **EDP Creation**: Natural England develops draft EDPs → 28-day public consultation → Secretary of State approval (must materially outweigh negative effects)
2. **Developer Use**: Online levy calculator; EDP replaces HRA or species licence; payment on agreed schedule
3. **Monitoring**: Annual reports; backup measures if targets at risk; SoS power to amend/revoke

## Initial Focus: Nutrient Neutrality

First EDPs address nutrient neutrality, currently blocking ~16,500 homes/year. Targeted catchments include Severn/Avon, Solent, Somerset Levels, The Broads, and others. Natural England's existing Nutrient Mitigation Scheme transitions into EDPs.

| Period | Actions |

## Future Scope

Government intends to expand to other protected species and habitats. Great crested newt District Level Licensing is a likely next phase.
