# Planning Obligations — Section 106 Guidance

**Source type**: National planning guidance (MHCLG)
**URL**: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/planning-obligations
**Legislation**: Section 106, Town and Country Planning Act 1990

## Summary

National guidance on planning obligations ("Section 106 agreements"). These are legally binding agreements between developers and local planning authorities to mitigate development impacts. They run with the land and are enforceable.

## The Three Tests

All S106 obligations must be:
1. **Necessary** to make the development acceptable in planning terms
2. **Directly related** to the development
3. **Fairly and reasonably related** in scale and kind to the development

(CIL Regulations 2010, Regulation 122; also NPPF)

## How S106 Is Used for Nature

Common nature-related S106 uses:
- Off-site BNG delivery where on-site provision is not feasible
- Habitat management and monitoring endowments
- Ecological mitigation and compensation for protected species
- Long-term management of retained or created habitats
- Contributions to nature reserves or green infrastructure

## Relationship with CIL

| S106 | CIL |
|------|-----|
| Site-specific, negotiated | Fixed charge per sq m |
| Directly related to the development | General area-wide infrastructure |
| Three statutory tests apply | Charging schedule approved by independent examiner |
| Biodiversity/nature mitigation | Broader infrastructure (roads, schools, green space) |

Both can be pooled for the same infrastructure if documented in infrastructure funding statements.
