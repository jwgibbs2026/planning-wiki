# Natural England — Great Crested Newts Planning Guidance

**Source type**: Natural England standing advice (gov.uk)
**URL**: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/great-crested-newts-advice-for-making-planning-decisions
**Ingested**: 2026-06-10

---

## What this source is

Natural England's standing advice for local planning authorities (LPAs) assessing planning applications that may affect great crested newts (GCN). As standing advice, it is a material planning consideration — LPAs must take it into account when determining applications.

## Key topics covered

- Legal protection of GCN as [[European Protected Species]] under the Habitats Regulations 2017
- GCN as a [[Species of Principal Importance]] under the NERC Act 2006
- [[District Level Licensing]] as an alternative to traditional site-by-site mitigation licensing
- Survey trigger thresholds (500m pond rule) and survey data currency requirements
- Impact assessment framework (High / Medium / Low)
- Mitigation and compensation requirements, including the 2-pond minimum rule
- Licensing obligations and the three-part test
- Planning conditions and coordination with Natural England

## Most important findings for planning practice

1. **500m survey trigger** — any suitable water body (pond or ditch) within 500m of a development triggers a survey requirement.
2. **4-season data currency** — survey data older than 4 survey seasons is not valid for planning purposes.
3. **2-pond minimum compensation** — if a breeding pond is destroyed, at least 2 new high-quality replacement ponds must be created and established *before* the original pond is destroyed.
4. **District Level Licensing** — a population-scale alternative to traditional EPS mitigation licences; developers pay into a scheme that creates habitat in strategically targeted areas.
5. **LPA coordination duty** — LPAs should confirm that Natural England will issue a licence before granting planning permission.

## Relationship to other sources in this wiki

This source is the second Natural England standing advice ingested, following Natural England - Bats Planning Guidance. Together they provide detailed procedural coverage of the two most commonly encountered European Protected Species in English planning. The structural parallels between bat and GCN planning obligations are strong — both involve survey triggers, impact frameworks, mitigation hierarchies, and EPS licensing via the three-part test.
