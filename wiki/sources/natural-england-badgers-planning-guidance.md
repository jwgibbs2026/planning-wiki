# Natural England — Badgers Planning Guidance

**Source type**: Natural England standing advice (gov.uk)
**URL**: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/badgers-surveys-and-mitigation-for-development-projects
**Ingested**: 2026-06-10

---

## What this source covers

Natural England's standing advice for local planning authorities (LPAs) on badger protection in planning applications. It covers the legal framework, survey requirements, impact assessment, avoidance and mitigation strategies, and the licensing process. It applies to England and forms part of the protected species planning advice collection.

---

## Key findings

1. **Badgers are protected under a dedicated Act** — the [[Protection of Badgers Act 1992]] — not under the Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981 or the Habitats Regulations. This is a structurally important distinction from bats, great crested newts, and otters.
2. **Surveys are triggered by habitat suitability or historical records**, with [[NBN Atlas]] recommended as the first check for distribution data.
3. **Avoidance is the priority**: layout design, lighting elimination near setts, and vegetation retention come before mitigation.
4. **Translocation is explicitly discouraged** due to bovine tuberculosis (bTB) transmission risks and welfare concerns — a unique constraint not present for any other species covered in this wiki.
5. **Survey confidentiality is required** to prevent persecution — a species-specific procedural requirement not standard for bats, GCN, or otters.
6. **Planning conditions must be coordinated with licensing** before permission is granted, mirroring the EPS licence/planning permission relationship but operating under a different legal framework.
7. **No District Level Licensing equivalent exists** for badgers — all licensing is site-by-site, as with otters.

## Relationship to other sources

This source is directly parallel in structure and status to:
- Natural England - Bats Planning Guidance
- Natural England - Great Crested Newts Planning Guidance
- Natural England - Otters Planning Guidance

The key distinction is that badger protection operates under a separate legislative framework (the Protection of Badgers Act 1992) rather than the European Protected Species framework under the Habitats Regulations. The three-part EPS test does **not** apply to badgers.
