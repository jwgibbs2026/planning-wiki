# GWT Bringing Nature Back — Strategy to 2030

**Source type**: Organisation strategy document
**URL**: https://www.gloucestershirewildlifetrust.co.uk/sites/default/files/2022-08/220606%20Glouc%20Strategy%20report%2022_web.pdf
**Published**: 2022

## Summary

GWT's strategy to stop and reverse wildlife declines in Gloucestershire by 2030. Sets out three goals and specific quantitative targets for land management, habitat creation, community engagement, and climate solutions.

## Key Statistics

- GWT manages 1,100 ha → target 1,700 ha by 2030
- Less than 1.5% of Cotswolds wildflower meadows remain (down from 40%)
- 70% of traditional orchards lost in Gloucestershire
- 28,000 members; hundreds of volunteers

## Three Goals and Key Targets

| Goal | Key 2030 Targets |
|------|-----------------|
| Stop/reverse wildlife declines | 1,700 ha managed; 30% of all Gloucestershire land for nature; 300 ha urban GI; 10 Nature Recovery Zones |
| More people taking action | 1 in 4 people in county; 25,000 school children; 15,000 people taking meaningful action |
| Normalise natural climate solutions | 2,000 ha woodland (Severn Treescapes); 300 ha new wetlands (Wildbelt); 5,000 ha farmland wildlife enhancement |

## Key Programmes

- **Severn Treescapes**: 3 million trees across Forest of Dean/Wye Valley to Wyre Forest; 1,700 tonnes carbon
- **Wildbelt**: 300 ha wetland on River Severn — natural flood management, biodiversity, wellbeing
- **Nature Recovery Zones**: 10 zones across county expanding from GWT reserves
- **Building with Nature**: GWT's development standard
