# Natural England — Hazel Dormice Planning Guidance

**Source type**: Natural England standing advice (material planning consideration)
**URL**: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/hazel-dormice-advice-for-making-planning-decisions
**Ingested**: 2026-06-10

## What this source is

This is Natural England's standing advice for local planning authorities (LPAs) on how to evaluate development applications that may affect hazel dormice. As standing advice, it is a material consideration in planning decisions. It covers legal protection, survey triggers, impact assessment, mitigation, licensing, and long-term monitoring.

---

## Key findings

**Legal status**: Hazel dormice are European Protected Species (EPS) under the Conservation of Habitats and Species Regulations 2010 (2017 consolidation) and additionally protected under the Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981. They are also listed as a [[Species of Principal Importance]] under the NERC Act 2006 and are classified as Vulnerable on England's Red List for Mammals.

**Survey triggers**: An ecological survey is required when historical records or distribution data suggest dormice presence, or when development affects woodland, hedgerow, or scrub habitat. The habitat-based trigger is relatively broad — the presence of suitable habitat alone is sufficient.

**Survey methods**: Methods range from visual nest and hazelnut searches (suitable for minor projects) to nest tubes, nest boxes, and footprint tracking tunnels for projects with significant potential impacts. See [[Dormouse Surveys]] for detail.

**Survey currency**: Surveys up to three years old are acceptable if habitats remain unchanged. This differs from the four-season rule applied to great crested newts.

**Presence assumption rule**: If dormice are found anywhere on a site, their presence must be assumed throughout all suitable habitat on that site. This has significant implications for survey scope and mitigation design.

**Mitigation hierarchy**: Habitat redesign and timing adjustments → habitat improvement (thinning, coppicing, planting) → nest box installation → green bridges connecting fragmented habitats → translocation as last resort.

**Green bridges**: Explicitly named as a mitigation tool for reconnecting fragmented dormouse populations across roads or other barriers. See [[Green Bridges]].

**Translocation**: A last-resort measure requiring specialist expertise, a suitable receptor site, and a licence from Natural England.

**Licensing**: Developers require an [[EPS Mitigation Licence]] before works affecting dormice can proceed. The standard three-part test applies. LPAs should coordinate planning conditions with Natural England's licensing requirements.

**Monitoring**: Long-term monitoring plans must track population status, vegetation management, foraging and nesting habitat availability, and mitigation effectiveness.
