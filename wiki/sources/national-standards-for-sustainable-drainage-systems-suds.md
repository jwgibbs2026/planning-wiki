# National Standards for Sustainable Drainage Systems (SuDS)

**Publisher**: MHCLG / Defra (gov.uk)
**Type**: Statutory guidance
**Status**: In force — applies to all new developments requiring planning permission in England (excluding trunk roads and retrofits)
**URL**: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/national-standards-for-sustainable-drainage-systems/national-standards-for-sustainable-drainage-systems-suds

---

## Summary

This statutory guidance sets out seven binding standards governing how surface water drainage must be designed for new development in England. It requires a "SuDS approach" that mimics natural drainage — managing runoff at or near source, close to the surface — rather than routing it underground through traditional pipe systems. Compliance must be demonstrated from the earliest planning stages.

Local Planning Authorities act as SuDS Approving Bodies. [[Lead Local Flood Authorities]] (LLFAs) provide flood risk and drainage information. Sewerage companies have been able to adopt SuDS since April 2020 under Ofwat's Code for Adoption Agreements.

---

## The Seven Standards

**Standard 1 — Runoff Destinations**: Surface water must discharge via the highest feasible priority in a strict hierarchy: reuse > infiltration > surface water bodies > surface water sewers > combined sewers. Higher cost alone is not a valid reason to use a lower-priority destination.

**Standard 2 — Everyday Rainfall (Interception)**: Systems must manage at least the first 5 mm of rainfall without runoff. Targets are 80% interception in summer (May–October) and 50% in winter (November–April).

**Standard 3 — Extreme Rainfall and Flooding**: Discharge must be limited to greenfield runoff rates (minimum 3 litres/second/hectare) for 50% AEP events, with controlled discharge matching greenfield conditions for 1% AEP events. Climate change must be designed for using the Environment Agency's Upper End Allowance factors.

**Standard 4 — Water Quality**: Robust water quality risk assessments are required. Management trains must address diffuse particulates and chemicals. Pre-treatment is required for infiltration systems. High-risk land uses may need proprietary treatment.

**Standard 5 — Amenity**: Designs must contribute to placemaking and environmental enhancement, include vegetation to combat urban heat, and support walking, cycling, recreation, and informal play where feasible.

**Standard 6 — Biodiversity**: SuDS must create diverse, resilient local ecosystems contributing to net biodiversity gains. Biodiversity risk and opportunity assessments are required. The mitigation hierarchy (avoid, mitigate, compensate) applies. SuDS habitats can contribute toward mandatory Biodiversity Net Gain calculations. Designs must support Local Nature Recovery Strategies and habitat connectivity.

**Standard 7 — Construction, Operation, Maintenance, and Structural Integrity**: Management and maintenance plans must support all other standards. Structural components must have a minimum design life matching the development lifetime. Monitoring provisions must be agreed with approving bodies for larger developments.

## Key Design Principles

- **Natural water management**: mimic natural drainage; manage runoff at or near source, as close to the surface as practicable
- **Early and integrated design**: surface water management must be considered from earliest site appraisal, integrated with topography, road layout, Green Infrastructure, biodiversity, and climate resilience
- **SuDS management train**: a series of drainage features working together near the runoff source to control flow, improve water quality, encourage biodiversity, and integrate into landscape design

---

## Regulatory Framework

- LPAs act as SuDS Approving Bodies; early engagement is required
- [[Lead Local Flood Authorities]] provide Local Flood Risk Management Strategies and Strategic Flood Risk Assessments
- Approved Document H (Building Regulations) applies within property curtilages
- Environmental Permitting Regulations govern discharges containing pollutants
- Mandatory 10% Biodiversity Net Gain (from February 2024) applies; SuDS habitats can count toward this target

---

## Key Design Notes

- Pumping is permitted only by exception where gravity drainage is impossible
- Base of infiltration features must remain 1 metre above maximum groundwater levels
- Previously developed (brownfield) sites may agree a relaxation of up to 5× greenfield runoff rates with evidence
- Contaminated land: higher-priority destinations should not be discounted where practicable remediation is possible

---

## Scope Limitations

- Excludes trunk roads and retrofit projects
- Applies to England only
