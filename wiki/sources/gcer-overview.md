# GCER — Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records

**Source type**: Organisation website
**URL**: https://www.gcer.co.uk/

## Summary

GCER is Gloucestershire's Local Environmental Records Centre (LERC), operating as part of the Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust. It holds over 2 million biological and environmental records for the county and provides data services to planners, ecologists, and communities.

## Key Facts

- Over 2 million records, continually updated
- Services: data searches, GIS analysis and mapping, nature recovery mapping, GIS training
- New pricing effective April 2026
- Underpins the Gloucestershire LNRS evidence base

## Role in Planning

GCER data searches are routinely required for:
- Ecological assessments supporting planning applications
- Strategic Environmental Assessments for Local Plans
- Habitat Regulations Assessments
- Neighbourhood Plan ecological evidence bases

GCER confirms presence/absence of protected species, local wildlife sites, and priority habitats within or near development sites.
