# Natural England — Water Voles Planning Guidance

**Source type**: Natural England standing advice (gov.uk)
**URL**: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/water-voles-surveys-and-mitigation-for-development-projects
**Last updated**: 7 April 2025
**Ingested**: 2026-06-10

---

## Summary

This is Natural England's standing advice for local planning authorities (LPAs) assessing planning applications that may affect water voles. It functions as a material planning consideration under England's protected species framework.

Water voles are protected under the Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981 — not under the Habitats Regulations European Protected Species (EPS) framework. This is a critical legal distinction: the three-part derogation test that applies to bats, great crested newts, and otters does not apply to water voles. A separate licensing regime applies, including a simplified Class Licence CL31 for small-scale impacts.

---

## Key topics covered

- Legal protection under WCA 1981 (non-EPS)
- Endangered status on England's mammal conservation list
- Species of Principal Importance (S41) designation
- Survey triggers: historical records, distribution data, suitable habitat indicators
- Habitat indicators: soft earth banks, slow-flowing water, vegetation, predator escape areas
- Precautionary principle: absence of records does not mean absence of species
- Development impact assessment requirements
- Mitigation hierarchy: avoidance > displacement > compensation > translocation (last resort)
- Displacement mitigation: vegetation removal to encourage relocation to connected habitat
- Compensation measures including mink control
- Licensing: standard mitigation licence and Class Licence CL31 (<50m bank)
- Post-development monitoring and management plans

---

## Most important findings for planning practice

1. **WCA 1981, not EPS** — water voles sit outside the Habitats Regulations tier; the three-part test does not apply
2. **Class Licence CL31** — a simplified licensing route for projects affecting less than 50 metres of bank; a practical tool not available for bats, GCN, otters, or badgers
3. **Habitat-based survey trigger** — specific, actionable indicators that LPAs and communities can apply
4. **Mink control as compensation** — an unusual species-specific compensation measure reflecting the ecological reality of water vole decline
5. **Translocation is last resort** — consistent with badger and otter guidance
