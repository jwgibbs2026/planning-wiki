# Gloucestershire Local Nature Recovery Strategy 2026

**Source type**: Statutory strategy
**URL**: https://www.gloucestershire.gov.uk/media/x4wf133n/gloucestershire-local-nature-recover-strategy-23-02-2026.pdf
**Published**: 23 February 2026
**Legislation**: Environment Act 2021

## Summary

Gloucestershire County Council's statutory LNRS — the first for the county under the Environment Act 2021. 84-page document mapping existing biodiversity and identifying opportunities for nature recovery. LPAs must "have regard to" it in planning decisions and Local Plans.

## Key Statistics

- 36,028 ha of woodland in Gloucestershire
- 75% of land in agricultural/horticultural use
- <1.5% of wildflower meadows remain in the Cotswolds
- 97% decline in lowland meadow nationally (1930–1984)
- 90% decline in coppiced woodland nationally (1900–1970)
- 11% urban land cover

## Internationally Designated Sites in Gloucestershire

- Wye Valley Woodlands SAC
- Walmore Common SPA
- Severn Estuary SAC/SPA/Ramsar
- Cotswold Beechwoods SAC
- Rodborough Common SAC
- Dixton Wood SAC

## Six Key Messages

1. Safeguarding existing biodiversity-rich sites
2. Landscape-scale connectivity: bigger, better, more, joined
3. Climate emergency — carbon sequestration and climate adaptation habitats
4. Our relationship with water — re-naturalisation, natural flood management, floodplain restoration
5. Value of mixed and wilder habitats — mosaic habitats, ecotones, conservation grazing
6. Biodiversity in developments and settlements — wildlife corridors, biodiverse SuDS, Building with Nature

## Priority Species

Gloucestershire strongholds: large blue butterfly, white-clawed crayfish, dormice, multiple bat species, curlew, adders, nightjars, rare ferns/bryophytes.

## Planning Use

- BNG off-site units should target LNRS priority habitat creation areas
- LPAs must "have regard to" LNRS in planning decisions
- New developments should incorporate wildlife corridors and biodiverse SuDS
- User guides available on LNRS website for BNG projects and community groups
