# Local Wildlife Sites — Natural Environment Planning Guidance

**Source type**: National planning practice guidance (PPG) — Natural Environment
**URL**: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/natural-environment

## Summary

Part of the government's Planning Practice Guidance on the natural environment. Sets out what Local Wildlife Sites are, how LPAs should designate and protect them, and how priority habitats (Section 41 habitats) interact with planning decisions.

## Local Wildlife Sites — Definition

Areas of substantive nature conservation value locally designated by LPAs and partnerships. Contribute to ecological networks and nature recovery. Non-statutory designation (unlike SSSIs) but carry significant weight in planning decisions.

## LPA Responsibilities

- Identify and map LWSs in development plans
- Include protective policies against harm or loss
- Enhance sites and their connections to ecological networks
- Establish partnerships with landowners and representative bodies

## Selection Criteria

Size/extent, diversity, naturalness, rarity, fragility, typicalness, recorded history, connectivity, educational value.

## Priority Habitats (Section 41, NERC Act 2006)

Statutory list of habitats and species of principal importance in England. LPAs must consider development impacts on Section 41 habitats. Includes: ancient woodland, species-rich grassland, lowland meadows, hedgerows, chalk streams, open mosaic habitats on brownfield land.

## Mitigation Hierarchy

1. Avoidance (preferred)
2. Mitigation
3. Compensation (last resort)

"Where a development cannot satisfy the requirements of the mitigation hierarchy, planning permission should be refused."

## LNRS Integration

LPAs must "have regard to" the Local Nature Recovery Strategy when making planning decisions and preparing Local Plans. LWSs often coincide with LNRS opportunity areas.
