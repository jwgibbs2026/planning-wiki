# GWT Nature Recovery Zones

**Source type**: Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust website
**URL**: https://www.gloucestershirewildlifetrust.co.uk/nature-recovery-zones

## Summary

GWT's Nature Recovery Zones are a strategic conservation framework extending wildlife protection beyond individual nature reserves by working with neighbouring landowners and farmers. They create connected "super reserves" — implementing the Lawton Report (2010) principle of "bigger, better, more, joined up."

## The Ten Zones

| Zone | Key Features |
|------|-------------|
| Cotswolds Escarpment | Limestone grasslands, ancient woodland |
| Severn Hams | Floodplain wetlands, wading birds |
| Windrush Valley | Floodplain meadows, river valley |
| Slad Valley | Limestone grassland, orchids, white-clawed crayfish |
| Stroud Commons | Grasslands, woodlands, bat habitats |
| The Golden Valley | Large blue butterflies, insect assemblages |
| Central Forest | Pine marten, diverse habitat mosaic |
| Lower Wye and Tidenham Chase | Ancient woodland, heathland |
| Leadon Valley | Arable farmland, wildlife fragments, wild daffodils |
| Lower Woods to Westonbirt | Ancient woodland, species-rich meadows |

## Planning Relevance

- BNG off-site units targeting zones maximise ecological coherence
