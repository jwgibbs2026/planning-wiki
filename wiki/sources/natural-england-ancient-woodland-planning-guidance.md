# Natural England - Ancient Woodland Planning Guidance

## What this source covers

This is joint standing advice from Natural England and the [[Forestry Commission]] for local planning authorities assessing planning applications that affect ancient woodland, ancient trees, or veteran trees in England. As standing advice, it is a material planning consideration — LPAs must take it into account when making decisions.

The guidance covers:
- Definitions of ancient woodland, ancient trees, and veteran trees
- The irreplaceability principle and its consequences for planning decisions
- Direct and indirect impacts of development
- The mitigation hierarchy (avoidance → mitigation → compensation)
- Quantified buffer zone requirements
- Evidence requirements (BS 5837 tree surveys, ecological surveys)
- Consultation roles (Natural England statutory; Forestry Commission non-statutory)

---

## Key findings for this wiki

1. **Irreplaceability is the central principle** — ancient woodland, ancient trees, and veteran trees cannot be recreated; compensation cannot alone justify approval
2. **The planning test is high** — permission should be refused unless "wholly exceptional reasons" exist AND a suitable compensation strategy is in place
3. **Buffer zones are quantified** — 15m minimum from woodland boundary; 15× trunk diameter or 5m from canopy edge for individual trees (whichever is larger)
4. **Both direct and indirect impacts must be assessed** — ecological severance, pollution, disturbance, and management changes are as material as physical destruction
5. **Translocation is explicitly unacceptable** — unlike some species mitigation, ancient woodland cannot be moved to another site
6. **BS 5837 tree surveys are required evidence** — desk study alone is insufficient

## Gloucestershire relevance

Ancient woodland is highly relevant to Gloucestershire:
- The **Forest of Dean** is one of England's largest ancient woodland complexes
- The **Cotswold escarpment** contains significant ancient woodland fragments
- The **Severn Vale** contains scattered ancient woodland remnants
- Ancient and veteran trees occur widely in hedgerows, parkland, and orchards across all six districts
