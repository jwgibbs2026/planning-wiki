# Forestry Commission

The Forestry Commission is the UK government's forestry authority for England (and, separately, Scotland). In the planning context, it acts as a **non-statutory consultee** for planning applications that affect ancient woodland.

---

## Role in planning

### Non-statutory consultee for ancient woodland

When a planning application would affect ancient woodland, the Forestry Commission is a non-statutory consultee. This means:

- The LPA is **not legally required** to consult the Forestry Commission (unlike statutory consultees such as Natural England)
- However, the Forestry Commission's advice is a **material planning consideration** — the LPA must take it into account if it is submitted
- The Forestry Commission's objections carry **less formal weight** than those of Natural England in the planning process
- Communities and objectors can reference Forestry Commission guidance in representations, but should also ensure Natural England is engaged where SSSI interests are present

### Contrast with Natural England

Natural England is the **statutory consultee** for planning applications affecting ancient woodland within or adjacent to SSSIs, and for other exceptional cases. Natural England's statutory role gives its advice greater formal weight in the planning process.

For ancient woodland outside SSSIs, the Forestry Commission is the primary specialist consultee, but its non-statutory status means LPAs have more discretion about how much weight to give its advice.

---

## Other planning-related functions

Beyond ancient woodland, the Forestry Commission is involved in:
- **Felling licences** — required for most tree felling in England; the Forestry Commission issues these
- **Forestry applications** — new woodland creation and restocking
- **PAWS restoration** — the Forestry Commission manages PAWS restoration programmes, particularly in the Forest of Dean
- **Environmental Impact Assessment** for forestry projects

---

## Gloucestershire context

The Forestry Commission manages significant areas of the **Forest of Dean** — one of England's largest ancient woodland complexes and a major feature of Gloucestershire's natural environment. The Forest of Dean includes areas of ancient semi-natural woodland, [[Plantations on Ancient Woodland Sites]], and ongoing restoration programmes.

The Forestry Commission's Forest of Dean district office is a relevant contact for planning applications and forestry matters in western Gloucestershire.
