# MHCLG — Ministry of Housing, Communities and Local Government

The Ministry of Housing, Communities and Local Government (MHCLG) is the central government department responsible for housing, planning policy, and local government in England. It is the primary author of the [[NPPF|National Planning Policy Framework]] and the suite of Planning Practice Guidance documents.

## Role in Planning

MHCLG is responsible for:
- **[[NPPF]]**: sets national planning policy; updated periodically (most recently 2024)
- **Planning legislation**: brings forward and implements Acts of Parliament affecting the planning system — including the Levelling Up and Regeneration Act 2023 and the Planning and Infrastructure Act 2025
- **Planning Practice Guidance (PPG)**: online guidance accompanying the NPPF on all planning topics
- **Community Infrastructure Levy**: sets the regulatory framework for CIL
- **Biodiversity Net Gain**: alongside [[Defra]], implements BNG through the planning system
- **[[SuDS|National SuDS Standards]]**: jointly authored with [[Defra]]; mandatory since April 2024

## Name History

The department has been known by several names in recent years due to machinery of government changes:
- **DCLG** — Department for Communities and Local Government (to 2018)
- **MHCLG** — Ministry of Housing, Communities and Local Government (2018–2021, then 2024–present)
- **DLUHC** — Department for Levelling Up, Housing and Communities (2021–2024)

Planning documents from any of these periods carry equal weight — the name change does not affect their status.

## Key Publications

- **National Planning Policy Framework** — the primary national planning policy document
- **Planning Practice Guidance** — online supplementary guidance on all planning topics
- **National Design Guide and National Model Design Code** — design standards for new development
- **National SuDS Standards** (joint with Defra) — statutory SuDS requirements from April 2024
