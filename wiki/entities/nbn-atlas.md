# NBN Atlas

The **National Biodiversity Network (NBN) Atlas** is the UK's primary national repository for biodiversity and species occurrence data. It aggregates records from hundreds of recording organisations, wildlife trusts, government bodies, and citizen science projects into a single, publicly searchable database, making them freely accessible online.

**Website**: https://nbnatlas.org

---

## Role in Planning

The NBN Atlas is the standard first-check data source for protected species records near a development site. Natural England's standing advice for protected species — including otters, bats, hazel dormice, great crested newts, and others — recommends checking the NBN Atlas as a first step when assessing whether a survey is required. Ecologists and LPAs use it to identify whether protected or notable species have been recorded within a defined radius of a proposed development, informing decisions about whether ecological survey is required.

The NBN Atlas is particularly useful for:

- Checking whether [[European Protected Species]] have been recorded near a development site
- Identifying [[Species of Principal Importance]] (S41 species) in the vicinity
- Providing a national baseline before consulting local sources

A positive record of a protected species within a relevant search radius is a key trigger for requiring an ecological survey. However, a search of the NBN Atlas does not substitute for a full ecological survey — absence of records does not mean absence of species, and surveys may still be required based on habitat suitability alone.

## Species Covered Relevant to Planning

The NBN Atlas holds records for all major protected species groups relevant to planning in England, including:

| Species group | Planning relevance |
|---|---|
| Bats | All species; EPS; see [[Bats in Planning]] |
| Great crested newts | EPS; 500m survey trigger; see [[Great Crested Newts in Planning]] |
| Otters | EPS; water proximity trigger; see [[Otters in Planning]] |
| Badgers | Protection of Badgers Act 1992; see [[Badgers in Planning]] |
| Water voles | WCA 1981 Schedule 5; see [[Water Voles in Planning]] |
| Wild birds | WCA 1981; see [[Wild Birds in Planning]] |
| Hazel dormice | EPS + S41; see [[Hazel Dormice in Planning]] |
| Ancient woodland | Habitat data; see [[Ancient Woodland in Planning]] |

## Relationship to GCER

The NBN Atlas provides national coverage but may not include all locally held records. The Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records (GCER) is the county-level repository and holds data that may not yet be aggregated into the national database. Best practice is to consult **both** sources:

- **NBN Atlas** — national picture, publicly accessible, free to use
- **GCER** — local detail, more complete for Gloucestershire, may require a data request

---

## Limitations

- Records may be historical and not reflect current species distribution; species distributions change over time
- Absence of records does not confirm absence of a species — surveys may still be required based on habitat suitability
- Data completeness varies by species and recording group; many species are under-recorded, particularly in less-surveyed areas
- Some sensitive records (e.g., badger sett locations) are withheld or shown at lower resolution to prevent disturbance or persecution
- NBN Atlas data should always be supplemented with local data from Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records for planning purposes

---

## Gloucestershire Context

For development proposals in Gloucestershire, the NBN Atlas should be used alongside Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records data. Parish councils and community groups can use the NBN Atlas to check what species have been recorded near proposed development sites before engaging with the planning process.
