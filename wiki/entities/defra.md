# Defra — Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs

The Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (Defra) is the UK central government department responsible for environmental protection, food production, the rural economy, and biodiversity policy in England. It is the lead department for the [[Environment Act 2021]] and the policy owner for [[Biodiversity Net Gain]], [[Local Nature Recovery Strategy|Local Nature Recovery Strategies]], and the wider nature-recovery framework.

Defra sets the statutory and policy framework that bodies such as [[Natural England]] and the [[Environment Agency]] then deliver on the ground. Its responsibilities relevant to planning include the design of the [[Biodiversity Metric]], the [[Nature Restoration Fund]] and Environmental Delivery Plans, agricultural transition and [[Agri-environment Schemes]], and — jointly with [[MHCLG]] — the National Standards for Sustainable Drainage Systems ([[SuDS]]).

## Gloucestershire Context

Defra policy shapes almost every nature-related planning requirement in Gloucestershire, from mandatory BNG on new development to the funding mechanisms (Countryside Stewardship, SFI, Landscape Recovery) that deliver the county's [[Local Nature Recovery Strategy]]. Defra is also the department behind the bovine TB control programme that affects [[Badgers in Planning|badger]] mitigation in the county.
