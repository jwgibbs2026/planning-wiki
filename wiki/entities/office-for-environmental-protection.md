# Office for Environmental Protection

The Office for Environmental Protection (OEP) is an independent watchdog established by the Environment Act 2021 to hold the government and public bodies in England to account for meeting environmental laws and commitments.

## Background

Before Brexit, the European Commission and European Court of Justice provided external oversight of how EU environmental law was implemented in the UK. The OEP was created to fill this role domestically following the UK's departure from the EU.

## What the OEP Does

- **Monitors progress** against environmental targets (including biodiversity targets under the Environment Act 2021)
- **Investigates complaints** from the public about public bodies failing to comply with environmental law
- **Takes enforcement action** against government departments and public bodies that breach environmental law
- **Advises** government on environmental law and policy
- **Reports annually** to Parliament on the state of environmental improvement plans

## Making a Complaint to the OEP

Members of the public can make a complaint to the OEP if they believe a public body (including a Local Planning Authority) has failed to comply with environmental law. This includes:
- Failures to comply with the Biodiversity Duty
- Failures to protect [[SSSI|SSSIs]]
- Failures to properly apply Habitats Regulations

The OEP is a last-resort mechanism — complainants must first exhaust available review processes.

## Limitations

The OEP cannot investigate decisions by local planning authorities on individual planning applications (that is the role of the courts). It focuses on systemic failures by public bodies to comply with environmental law.
