# Lead Local Flood Authorities (LLFAs)

Lead Local Flood Authorities (LLFAs) are upper-tier or unitary local authorities responsible for managing local flood risk in England. They play a specific and important role in the planning system, particularly in relation to [[SuDS|Sustainable Drainage Systems (SuDS)]].

---

## Role and Responsibilities

LLFAs are responsible for:

- **Managing local flood risk** from surface water, groundwater, and ordinary watercourses
- **Producing Local Flood Risk Management Strategies** — documents setting out how local flood risk will be managed across the authority area
- **Producing Strategic Flood Risk Assessments** — planning documents used by Local Planning Authorities to inform development decisions
- **Providing flood risk and drainage information** to developers and LPAs for planning applications
- **Engaging with developers** on drainage design, particularly for developments that may affect local flood risk

Under the national standards for [[SuDS]], developers must engage with LLFAs at an early stage to obtain flood risk and drainage information relevant to their site. This engagement helps identify local strategies, constraints, and requirements that must be reflected in drainage design.

---

## Relationship to LPAs and SuDS

Local Planning Authorities act as SuDS Approving Bodies — they assess whether drainage designs comply with the national standards. LLFAs are a distinct body that provides the flood risk and drainage context within which LPAs make those assessments.

In some areas, the LPA and LLFA are the same organisation (unitary authorities). In two-tier areas (like Gloucestershire), they are separate: the district council is the LPA, while the county council is the LLFA.

---

## Gloucestershire Context

**[[Gloucestershire County Council]]** is the Lead Local Flood Authority for Gloucestershire. It is responsible for:

- The Gloucestershire Local Flood Risk Management Strategy
- Strategic Flood Risk Assessments used by the six Gloucestershire district LPAs
- Providing drainage and flood risk information to developers across the county

Gloucestershire has significant flood risk, particularly in the Severn Vale, around Gloucester and Tewkesbury, and in the lower Avon and Severn catchments. Developers in these areas must engage with Gloucestershire County Council as LLFA at an early stage in the planning process.

The six Gloucestershire district councils — Cheltenham Borough Council, Gloucester City Council, Stroud District Council, Forest of Dean District Council, Tewkesbury Borough Council, and Cotswolds District Council — act as SuDS Approving Bodies in their respective areas, but rely on the county council's flood risk information and strategies.
