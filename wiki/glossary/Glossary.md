# Glossary

Plain-English definitions of key planning and ecology terms. Click any term to go to its full wiki page.

---

**[[Biodiversity]]**
The variety and variability of life on Earth — from genes and species to ecosystems and landscapes.

**Biodiversity Action Plan (BAP)**
A local plan (not a statutory document) for parish councils to protect and restore the natural environment. Also called a Local Nature Action Plan (LNAP).

**Biodiversity Duty**
A legal requirement on public bodies to consider, plan for, and report on what they are doing to conserve and enhance biodiversity.

**Biodiversity Net Gain (BNG)**
An approach to development that leaves biodiversity in a measurably better state than before. The current minimum requirement for new developments in England is 10%.

**[[Biodiversity Gain Plan]] (BGP)**
The statutory document a developer must submit and the LPA must approve before development can commence, demonstrating how the mandatory 10% (or higher) BNG requirement will be met and maintained for 30 years.

**BS 5837**
British Standard 5837:2012 — *Trees in relation to design, demolition and construction*. The mandatory tree survey methodology for planning applications affecting trees. Requires a Tree Survey, Constraints Plan, Tree Protection Plan, and Arboricultural Impact Assessment. Specifies Root Protection Area calculations and buffer zone distances. Referenced in [[Ancient Woodland in Planning]] and [[Tree Preservation Orders]].

**Building with Nature (BwN)**
A set of 12 standards defining what good Green Infrastructure looks like, plus an accreditation award for qualifying developments.

**Community Infrastructure Levy (CIL)**
A charge on new developments used to fund local infrastructure. Parishes with a made Neighbourhood Development Plan receive 25% of CIL receipts from development in their area.

**Conservation of Habitats and Species Regulations 2010**
UK domestic law implementing the EU Habitats Directive. Protects European Protected Species and designated habitats.

**Councillor**
An elected representative at parish, district, borough, or county level.

**CROW Act 2000**
The Countryside and Rights of Way Act 2000 — introduced the right to roam over open access land and strengthened [[SSSI]] protections.

**Environment Act 2021**
The UK's post-Brexit environmental protection framework; introduced mandatory Biodiversity Net Gain, the strengthened Biodiversity Duty, and the Office for Environmental Protection.

**Green Infrastructure (GI)**
A network of multi-functional green and blue spaces and features that delivers benefits for nature, people, and climate simultaneously.

**Hedgerows Regulations 1997**
Protects important rural hedgerows from removal without permission from the Local Planning Authority.

**Local Green Space (LGS)**
A designation communities can apply to locally valued green areas through a Neighbourhood Development Plan, giving protection equivalent to Green Belt.

**Local Nature Recovery Strategy (LNRS)**
A statutory spatial strategy identifying priorities for nature recovery at county level, required by the Environment Act 2021.

**Local Plan**
A statutory planning document produced by a Local Planning Authority setting out the spatial strategy, policies, and allocations for an area.

**Local Planning Authority (LPA)**
The body responsible for planning decisions — typically a district or borough council.

**Local Wildlife Sites (LWS)**
Locally designated (non-statutory) sites of ecological importance, identified by Local Environmental Record Centres such as the Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records.

**Major development**
Any residential application for 10+ dwellings, residential development on 0.5 ha or more, commercial floorspace of 1,000 sq m or more, or development on sites over 1 ha.

**Natural England**
The government's independent adviser on the natural environment; notifies [[SSSI|SSSIs]], issues Protected Species licences, and produces the Biodiversity Metric.

**Nature Recovery Network (NRN)**
A national framework of connected wildlife-rich areas aimed at restoring nature across 30% of England's land by 2030.

**Neighbourhood Development Plan (NDP)**
A statutory community-produced planning document enabling parishes and towns to set planning policies for their local area.

**[[NPPF]]**
The National Planning Policy Framework — the government's national planning policy document for England.

**Office for Environmental Protection (OEP)**
The independent watchdog that holds government and public bodies to account for meeting environmental laws.

**Parish Council**
The most local tier of local government; can produce NDPs, BAPs, and community groups to support nature.

**Planning Application**
A formal request to a Local Planning Authority for permission to carry out development.

**Planning Inspectorate**
An executive agency that examines Local Plans, handles appeals, and recommends decisions on nationally significant infrastructure.

**Protected Species**
Animals and plants that receive specific legal protection under the Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981 or Conservation of Habitats and Species Regulations 2010.

**[[SSSI]]**
Site of Special Scientific Interest — the most important category of wildlife site in England, designated by Natural England.

**[[SuDS]]**
Sustainable Urban Drainage Systems — drainage features that manage surface water to mimic natural processes, reducing flood risk and creating habitat.

**Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981**
The primary legislation protecting wildlife in Britain — covering wild birds, animals, plants, and [[SSSI|SSSIs]].
