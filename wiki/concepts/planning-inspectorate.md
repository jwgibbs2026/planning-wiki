# Planning Inspectorate

The Planning Inspectorate is an executive agency of the UK Government. It acts as an independent body that sits above local decision-making in the planning system.

## Key Responsibilities

- **Examining Local Plans** — Before a Local Plan can be adopted, it must be examined by an independent Inspector appointed by the Inspectorate. The Inspector checks that the plan is "sound" (positively prepared, justified, effective, and consistent with the [[NPPF]]).
- **Handling planning appeals** — When a Local Planning Authority refuses a Planning Application (or fails to decide in time), applicants can appeal to the Inspectorate.
- **Nationally Significant Infrastructure Projects** — The Inspectorate examines applications for major national infrastructure (roads, railways, energy projects) and makes recommendations to the Secretary of State.

## What "Sound" Means for Nature

During Local Plan examinations, the Inspector scrutinises whether the plan's environmental policies (including Biodiversity Net Gain, Green Infrastructure, and protections for [[SSSI|SSSIs]] and Local Wildlife Sites) are robust, evidence-based, and consistent with national policy. This makes the examination stage a critical point for ensuring ambitious nature policies survive.
