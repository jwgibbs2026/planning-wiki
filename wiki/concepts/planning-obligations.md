# Planning Obligations (Section 106)

Planning obligations — commonly called "Section 106 agreements" or "S106" — are legally binding agreements between developers and local planning authorities used to mitigate the impacts of development. They are created under Section 106 of the Town and Country Planning Act 1990.

## What Are They?

S106 agreements can require a developer to:
- Pay a financial contribution to the LPA for infrastructure or services
- Carry out specific works on or off the development site
- Restrict or control use of the land in a particular way
- Provide affordable housing

They run with the land — meaning any owner of the land in future is also bound by the agreement.

## The Three Tests

An S106 obligation can only be used to justify granting permission if it passes all three tests:

1. **Necessary** to make the development acceptable in planning terms
2. **Directly related** to the development
3. **Fairly and reasonably related** in scale and kind to the development

(Regulation 122, CIL Regulations 2010; NPPF)

## Use for Biodiversity and Nature

S106 is a key mechanism for securing:
- **Off-site Biodiversity Net Gain (BNG)**: where on-site delivery is impractical, developers fund off-site habitat creation
- **Habitat management endowments**: long-term funding for managing protected or created habitats
- **Ecological mitigation**: compensation for impacts on Protected Species or Local Wildlife Sites
- **Green infrastructure**: funding for parks, wildlife corridors, and accessible greenspace
- **Monitoring obligations**: ensuring mitigation measures are implemented and effective

## Relationship with CIL

| S106 | CIL |
|------|------|
| Negotiated, site-specific | Fixed charge per sq m of development |
| Must pass the three tests | Set by charging schedule |
| Any development where impacts arise | Most developments ≥100 sq m or new dwellings |
| Nature mitigation, affordable housing | General infrastructure (roads, schools, green space) |

Both can be pooled for the same piece of infrastructure, documented in annual Infrastructure Funding Statements.

## Future Changes

The Levelling Up and Regeneration Act 2023 includes provisions for a new Infrastructure Levy to eventually replace CIL and S106, though the transition timeline remains uncertain.

## Gloucestershire Context

In Gloucestershire, S106 agreements are used by all six district LPAs to:
- Secure off-site BNG habitat in areas identified by the Local Nature Recovery Strategy
- Require contributions to GCER for ongoing monitoring of ecological impacts
- Fund nature reserve enhancements through Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust
