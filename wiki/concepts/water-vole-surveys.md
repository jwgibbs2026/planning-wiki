# Water Vole Surveys

Water vole surveys are required when a development site contains or is adjacent to suitable riparian habitat, or when historical records indicate possible presence. Surveys must be carried out by a qualified ecologist meeting CIEEM competency standards.

---

## When surveys are required

Natural England advises that surveys should be requested by the LPA when **either** of the following applies:

- **Historical records or distribution data** suggest water voles may be present — check [[NBN Atlas]] and Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records as a first step
- **Suitable habitat** is present within or adjacent to the site

> **Key principle**: Absence of a record does not mean there are no water voles. If habitat is suitable, surveys are required regardless of whether records exist.

---

## Suitable habitat indicators

The following features indicate potential water vole habitat:

| Indicator | Significance |
|---|---|
| Soft earth banks | Suitable for burrow construction |
| Slow-flowing or still water | Rivers, streams, ditches, ponds, canals |
| Dense bankside vegetation | Rushes, sedges, grasses — food and cover |
| Predator escape areas | Vegetation structure allowing rapid retreat |

---

## Survey methods

Water vole surveys typically involve:

- **Habitat assessment** — evaluating bankside features against the indicators above
- **Field signs search** — looking for latrines (droppings), feeding stations (cut vegetation), burrow entrances, and runways through vegetation
- **Presence/absence determination** — confirming whether water voles are using the site

### Seasonality

Natural England's standing advice does not specify optimal survey seasons for water voles (unlike bat or GCN guidance, which have defined seasonal windows). CIEEM guidelines are the reference source for survey timing. In practice, surveys are most productive when vegetation is not too dense — late spring and autumn are generally favoured, but practitioners should consult current CIEEM guidance.

---

## Surveyor competency

Surveys must be carried out by a **qualified ecologist** meeting the competency standards set by the **Chartered Institute of Ecology and Environmental Management (CIEEM)**. This is consistent with the competency requirements across all Natural England protected species standing advice.

---

## Combined surveys with otters

In practice, riparian surveys often cover both [[Otters in Planning|otters]] and water voles simultaneously, as both species use similar habitats (slow-flowing watercourses with dense bankside vegetation). Developers and their ecologists should consider commissioning combined riparian mammal surveys to reduce costs and survey visits.

---

## Gloucestershire context

Before commissioning surveys, check:

1. [[NBN Atlas]] — national biodiversity records for water voles near the site
2. Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records (GCER) — county-level distribution data, which may include more recent or localised records than the national database
