# Natural England GI Framework

Natural England has published a Green Infrastructure Framework for England, setting out 15 principles that underpin high-quality GI planning, design, and management.

## The 15 Principles

The principles are organised around three themes:

### Why (the benefits)
1. Support nature recovery and Biodiversity Net Gain
2. Adapt to and mitigate climate change
3. Improve people's physical and mental health and wellbeing
4. Increase access to nature
5. Support sustainable economic growth

### What (the types and qualities)
6. Create a network of diverse, high-quality GI
7. Incorporate multiple functions and benefits
8. Prioritise irreplaceable assets (ancient woodland, rivers, etc.)
9. Integrate with the built environment (green roofs, walls, streets)
10. Include both accessible and wilder spaces

### How (the process)
11. Plan across boundaries and at multiple scales
12. Use evidence-based approaches and the LERC data
13. Ensure long-term management and maintenance
14. Co-design with communities
15. Monitor outcomes and adapt

## Relationship to Building with Nature

The Natural England GI Framework and Building with Nature are complementary, not competing. The NE framework provides strategic principles; BwN provides a practical accreditation standard for developers to demonstrate compliance.

## Relevance to Planning

Local Plans are expected to reflect the NE GI Framework in their GI policies. It provides a robust, government-endorsed reference for community groups and planners when assessing whether new development is truly delivering good GI.
