# Wye Valley National Landscape

The Wye Valley National Landscape (formerly the Wye Valley Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty) is the designated protected landscape covering the western edge of Gloucestershire and the Wye Valley corridor. It was designated in 1971 and is one of England and Wales's most biodiverse and visually distinctive landscapes.

## Designation and Geography

- **Designated**: 1971
- **Area**: approximately 326 sq km (126 sq miles)
- **Countries**: England (Gloucestershire, Herefordshire) and Wales (Monmouthshire)
- **Management**: joint management between English and Welsh authorities; Wye Valley AONB Joint Advisory Committee

The Gloucestershire portion covers primarily the lower Wye gorge, Tidenham Chase, and the limestone escarpment above the River Wye. It is distinct from the [[Cotswolds National Landscape]] and has its own management plan and planning policies.

## The Forest of Dean

The Forest of Dean is not a National Landscape designation — it is a **statutory Crown Forest**, one of the oldest in England, managed by the Forestry Commission under the Forestry Act 1967. The Forest of Dean District Council area shares a boundary with the Wye Valley National Landscape but the forest itself has no AONB/NL designation.

The Forest of Dean has significant ecological importance independently:
- Large area of ancient woodland including Plantations on Ancient Woodland Sites (PAWS) being restored
- Important populations of pine marten (reintroduced), wild boar, peregrine falcon, and veteran oaks
- Managed by [[Forestry Commission]] with commercial timber production alongside conservation objectives
- Part of GWT's Central Forest Nature Recovery Zone

## Planning Protections

The Wye Valley National Landscape carries the same high level of planning protection as other National Landscapes under the [[NPPF]]:
- **[[Seek to Further Duty]]**: relevant authorities must seek to further the purposes of the designation (s.85 CRoW Act 2000 / s.245 LURA 2023)
- **[[Sandford Principle]]**: conservation purpose takes priority over enjoyment when they conflict
- **[[Major Development in AONBs]]**: major development requires the exceptional circumstances test (NPPF paras 190–191)
- Development that would harm the special qualities of the landscape will be resisted

## Ecological Character

The Wye Valley National Landscape is internationally significant for:
- **Ancient woodland**: the Wye Valley Woodlands SAC (a Special Area of Conservation) runs through the landscape — one of the finest examples of beech, lime, and yew woodland in Europe
- **Limestone grassland**: flower-rich limestone meadows and cliff-face habitats
- **Bats**: Greater and lesser horseshoe bats, barbastelle — all highly sensitive to lighting
- **White-clawed crayfish**: the River Wye is a SAC partly qualifying for this species; American signal crayfish pose a serious threat
- **Peregrine, raven, and other cliff-nesting birds**
- **Veteran and ancient oaks** throughout the former wood pasture landscape

## Gloucestershire Planning Relevance

The Wye Valley National Landscape is a material consideration for planning applications in:
- Forest of Dean District — particularly the western parishes adjacent to the Wye gorge
- Any development with landscape or ecological effects visible from or reaching into the NL

Forest of Dean District Council must apply the Seek to Further Duty to all its planning decisions.
