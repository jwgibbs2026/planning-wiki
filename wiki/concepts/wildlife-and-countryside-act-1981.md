# Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981

The Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981 (WCA 1981) is the primary wildlife protection legislation in Great Britain. It provides legal protection for wild birds, animals, and plants, and underpins much of the protected species framework in the planning system.

---

## Key provisions relevant to planning

### Part I — Protection of birds
All wild birds, their nests, and eggs are protected under Part I of WCA 1981. It is an offence to:
- Kill, injure, or take any wild bird
- Damage or destroy a nest in use or being built
- Take or destroy the egg of any wild bird
- Possess a wild bird, its eggs, or parts
- Disturb a Schedule 1 bird at or near an active nest, or disturb dependent young

**Schedule 1** lists species receiving enhanced disturbance protection. Surveys for Schedule 1 species require a licensed ecologist.

**No development licence exists for wild birds.** Natural England cannot issue licences permitting construction harm to birds. See [[Wild Birds in Planning]].

### Part I — Schedule 5 (protected animals)
Schedule 5 lists animals (other than birds) that receive protection against killing, injuring, taking, and possession. Key planning-relevant Schedule 5 species include:
- **Water voles** — see [[Water Voles in Planning]]
- Great crested newts (also EPS under Habitats Regulations)
- Dormice (also EPS)
- White-clawed crayfish

### Part I — Schedule 8 (protected plants)
Schedule 8 lists plant species protected against picking, uprooting, and destruction. Less commonly triggered in planning than animal protections.

### Part II — SSSIs
WCA 1981 Part II established the framework for Sites of Special Scientific Interest ([[SSSI]]). The CROW Act 2000 subsequently strengthened SSSI protections.

## Practical significance in planning

WCA 1981 is relevant to virtually every development site in England. Key practical implications:
1. **Wild birds** — nesting season avoidance (March–August) applies to all sites with vegetation, buildings, or trees
2. **Schedule 1 birds** — specialist surveys required; licensed ecologist needed
3. **Water voles** — habitat-based survey triggers; Class Licence CL31 available for small impacts
4. **Schedule 5 animals** — presence triggers survey and mitigation requirements

## Gloucestershire context

WCA 1981 protections are engaged across Gloucestershire's diverse habitats. The county's agricultural landscape, woodland, wetlands, and urban areas all support species protected under the Act. Key local triggers include barn owl surveys for rural conversions, water vole surveys along Severn tributaries, and Schedule 1 bird surveys in the Forest of Dean and Cotswold escarpment.
