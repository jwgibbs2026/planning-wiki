# Local Planning Authority

A Local Planning Authority (LPA) is the body responsible for planning decisions in a given area. In most of England, the LPA is the district or borough council.

## What an LPA Does

- Produces and maintains the Local Plan
- Decides planning applications
- Enforces planning conditions and permissions
- Consults with statutory bodies (such as Natural England) on applications that may affect protected sites or species
- Must fulfil the Biodiversity Duty under the Environment Act 2021

## Nature and the LPA

LPAs must ensure planning applications comply with national and local biodiversity policies. This includes requiring Biodiversity Net Gain, protecting [[SSSI|SSSIs]] and Local Wildlife Sites, and ensuring that Protected Species are not harmed.

LPAs do not always proactively request ecological surveys — this is where community input and reports to the Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records can make a difference.
