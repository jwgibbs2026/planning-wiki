# Environmental Land Management Schemes

Environmental Land Management (ELM) schemes are the primary mechanism through which farmers and land managers in England are paid to deliver public goods — including nature recovery, carbon sequestration, water quality improvement, and heritage management. They replaced the EU Common Agricultural Policy (CAP) agri-environment schemes following Brexit.

---

## The three ELM tiers

ELM comprises three tiers:
1. **Sustainable Farming Incentive (SFI)** — broad and shallow; open to most farmers; covers soil health, hedgerows, arable field margins, and other actions
2. **Countryside Stewardship (CS)** — targeted; higher-tier agreements for more complex habitat management; includes capital grants
3. **Landscape Recovery** — large-scale, long-term projects for transformative land use change; relevant to nature recovery networks

---

## Importance for nature recovery in the CNL

The CNL Management Plan 2025–2030 describes ELM schemes as the **most important single mechanism for nature recovery in the wider countryside** of the [[Cotswolds National Landscape]]. The plan requires that ELM schemes operating within the CNL include measures from the [[Cotswolds Nature Recovery Plan]] and the relevant Local Nature Recovery Strategies.

Key ELM-relevant statistics for the CNL:
- Agri-environment scheme area: 64,500 Ha (£14,001,800 per year)
- 87% of CNL land is agricultural (49% arable, 43% grassland)
- Farming worth ~£800m/year to the CNL economy

---

## ELM and the PLTOF

PLTOF Target 5 requires 65–80% of land managers to adopt nature-friendly farming on 10–15% of their land by 2030. ELM schemes are the primary delivery mechanism for this target. Many PLTOF indicators related to nature-friendly farming are still in development.

---

## ELM and heritage

ELM schemes are also a mechanism for heritage management in the CNL. [[Historic England]] advises on environmental land management schemes in relation to heritage assets. PLTOF Target 10 (reducing heritage assets at risk) is partly dependent on ELM delivery.

---

## Defra's role

Defra is responsible for ELM scheme design and funding. The CNL Management Plan asks Defra to ensure that ELM and rural development support mechanisms are appropriate for the CNL and targeted towards LNRS delivery and PLTOF targets.

---

## Gloucestershire context

ELM schemes are available to farmers across Gloucestershire, including within the [[Cotswolds National Landscape]]. The Gloucestershire LNRS (being prepared by [[Gloucestershire County Council]]) will identify priority areas for ELM targeting. The [[Cotswolds Nature Recovery Plan]] provides the evidence base for ELM priorities within the CNL.
