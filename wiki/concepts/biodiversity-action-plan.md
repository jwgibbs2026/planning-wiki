# Biodiversity Action Plan

A Biodiversity Action Plan (BAP) — sometimes called a Local Nature Action Plan (LNAP) — is a document that sets out how a parish council or community will support biodiversity in its area.

## What Is It?

A BAP is not a statutory planning document. It does not carry the same legal weight as a Neighbourhood Development Plan or Local Plan. However, it is a valuable tool for:
- Setting clear targets for nature recovery at a local level
- Giving the community a shared vision and action plan
- Supporting the Biodiversity Duty that parish councils must fulfil
- Providing evidence for NDPs and Local Plans
- Guiding grant applications and community fundraising

## What Should a BAP Include?

- A review of the habitats and species found in the parish
- An assessment of threats and pressures on local nature
- Specific, measurable targets (e.g. "create 2 hectares of wildflower meadow by 2028")
- A set of actions with responsible parties and timescales
- A monitoring and review plan
- Consultation with the local community

## The Community Process

BAPs should involve the community through consultations, surveys, and events. This helps to:
- Gather local knowledge about wildlife
- Build community ownership of the plan
- Develop community groups to carry out actions (such as the example of Nailsworth Wildlife Group, which was formed following a BAP process)

## Getting Support

Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust can support parish councils in developing a BAP. GWT can provide ecological expertise, examples of good practice, and connections to the wider Nature Recovery Network.
