# NPPF — National Planning Policy Framework

The National Planning Policy Framework (NPPF) is the government's statement of national planning policy for England. All Local Plans and planning application decisions must be consistent with it.

## What Is It?

The NPPF is a document published by central government that sets out the planning policies that apply across England. It is not legislation — it is policy guidance — but it carries significant weight in planning decisions. The NPPF is periodically updated; the most recent version was published in 2024.

## Chapter 15: Conserving and Enhancing the Natural Environment

Chapter 15 is the key section for nature and biodiversity. It requires that planning policies and decisions:

- Protect and enhance biodiversity and geodiversity
- Identify and map [[SSSI|SSSIs]], Local Wildlife Sites, wildlife corridors, and ecological networks
- Protect ancient woodland, ancient and veteran trees
- Refuse permission for developments that would result in the loss of irreplaceable habitats unless there are wholly exceptional reasons
- Require all developments to deliver Biodiversity Net Gain
- Provide net gains for biodiversity where possible

## Green Infrastructure

The NPPF also requires Local Plans to plan for a network of multi-functional Green Infrastructure, including green roofs, SuDS, trees, parks, and wildlife habitats.

## The Presumption in Favour of Sustainable Development

The NPPF sets out a "presumption in favour of sustainable development" — meaning that development should generally be approved unless its impacts significantly outweigh its benefits. This presumption is balanced against the need to protect the natural environment; developments that harm irreplaceable habitats or protected species face a high bar.

## Using the NPPF in Your Response

When commenting on a Planning Application, you can directly quote or reference NPPF policies. Phrases like *"contrary to paragraph X of the NPPF"* carry weight with planning officers and committee members.
