# District Level Licensing

District Level Licensing (DLL) is an alternative to traditional site-by-site [[EPS Mitigation Licence|EPS mitigation licensing]] for great crested newts (GCN). Instead of requiring each developer to design and deliver on-site mitigation, DLL operates at a population scale — developers pay into a scheme that creates and enhances GCN habitat in strategically targeted locations across a district or county.

---

## How DLL Works

Under a traditional EPS mitigation licence, a developer must:
1. Survey for GCN
2. Design site-specific mitigation (habitat creation, translocation, etc.)
3. Apply to Natural England for a licence
4. Implement and monitor the mitigation

Under DLL, the process is simplified:
1. The developer checks whether their site falls within a DLL scheme area
2. If it does, they pay a **conservation payment** into the scheme
3. The scheme operator (typically a conservation body working with Natural England) uses the pooled funds to create or enhance GCN habitat at **strategically selected receptor sites** — locations identified as having the greatest potential to benefit GCN populations
4. The developer receives a licence (or licence coverage) without needing to design or deliver on-site mitigation

---

## Why DLL Can Deliver Better Outcomes

Traditional site-by-site mitigation often results in small, isolated habitat patches that may not be ecologically viable in the long term. DLL allows habitat creation to be:
- **Strategically located** — in areas identified by habitat mapping and population data as most beneficial for GCN
- **At scale** — larger, better-connected habitat patches that support viable populations
- **Coordinated** — aligned with Local Nature Recovery Strategy priorities and existing wildlife networks

This population-level logic is similar to the strategic approach underpinning Biodiversity Net Gain — both prioritise landscape-scale outcomes over piecemeal on-site measures.

---

## Developer Benefits

- Reduced survey burden in some cases (presence/absence surveys may still be required to confirm the site is within the scheme area)
- No requirement to design complex on-site mitigation
- Faster and more predictable licensing process
- Conservation payment provides certainty of cost

---

## Relationship to the Three-Part Test

DLL does not remove the requirement to satisfy the [[EPS Mitigation Licence|three-part licensing test]] — the scheme as a whole must demonstrate that:
1. There is an overriding public interest
2. There is no satisfactory alternative
3. Favourable conservation status of GCN will be maintained

However, because DLL schemes are pre-approved at a strategic level, individual developers within the scheme area benefit from this pre-existing justification.

## Gloucestershire Context

> **Knowledge gap**: Whether any Gloucestershire districts participate in a DLL scheme is not confirmed by this source. Natural England operates DLL schemes in a number of English counties, but geographic coverage is not universal. This is a significant practical question for developers and LPAs in Gloucestershire — if DLL is not available, traditional site-by-site licensing applies.

The Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records may hold information on whether GCN distribution data has been used to inform any DLL scheme boundary in the county. LPAs and developers should check directly with Natural England for current DLL availability.
