# Protection of Badgers Act 1992

The Protection of Badgers Act 1992 is the primary legislation protecting badgers and their setts in England, Scotland, and Wales. It is a **dedicated Act** for a single species group — structurally distinct from the broader wildlife protection framework of the Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981 and the European Protected Species (EPS) framework under the Conservation of Habitats and Species Regulations 2017.

---

## What the Act protects

The Act protects both the animal and its sett. It is unlawful to:

- Take, injure, ill-treat, or kill a badger, or attempt to do so
- Treat a badger cruelly
- Obstruct, disturb, damage, or destroy a badger sett or any part of it
- Cause a dog to enter a badger sett
- Sell, possess, or control a badger or any part of one (alive or dead)
- Mark, tag, or ring a badger without a licence

A **badger sett** is any structure or place used for badger dwelling — including tunnels, chambers, and entrances. The protection applies whether or not badgers are currently present, provided the sett is in use or has been recently used.

---

## Relationship to other wildlife legislation

| Feature | Protection of Badgers Act 1992 | Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981 | Habitats Regulations (EPS) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Species covered | Badgers only | Wide range of species | European Protected Species (bats, GCN, otters, etc.) |
| Sett/roost/habitat protection | Yes (setts) | Yes (roosts, nests) | Yes (breeding sites, resting places) |
| Three-part licensing test | No | No | Yes |
| Licensing body | Natural England | Natural England | Natural England |
| District Level Licensing | No | N/A | Yes (GCN only) |

The key practical implication for planning is that the **three-part EPS test** — which requires demonstrating no satisfactory alternative, imperative reasons of overriding public interest, and maintenance of favourable conservation status — does **not** apply to badgers. Badger licensing operates under a different set of criteria.

---

## Licensing

Where development would otherwise breach the Act, a **badger licence** from Natural England is required. Licences can be granted for development purposes, but:

- There is no District Level Licensing equivalent — all badger licensing is site-by-site
- Planning conditions must be coordinated with licence requirements before permission is granted
- Natural England's licensing capacity must be confirmed before permission is granted

---

## Planning relevance

The Act is a material consideration in planning decisions. LPAs must satisfy themselves that:
1. A badger survey has been carried out where triggered
2. The impact on badgers and setts has been properly assessed
3. Avoidance and mitigation measures are secured by condition
4. Any necessary badger licence from Natural England can be obtained

See [[Badgers in Planning]] for the full procedural guidance.

---

## Gloucestershire context

The Protection of Badgers Act 1992 applies across all six Gloucestershire districts. Given the county's extensive pastoral farmland, ancient woodland, and hedgerow networks, badger setts are common across the county. All LPAs should routinely screen planning applications for badger survey triggers.
