# Local Green Space

Local Green Space (LGS) is a designation that communities can use to protect locally valued green areas through their Neighbourhood Development Plan or Local Plan. Once designated, an LGS is protected from development in a way comparable to Green Belt.

## What Can Be Designated?

To be designated as LGS, a green area must be:
- **Local in character** — not an extensive tract of land
- **In reasonably close proximity** to the community it serves
- **Special to the community** for one of these reasons:
  - Beauty or tranquillity
  - Historic significance
  - Recreational value
  - Richness of wildlife
  - Important as green space for the community

## How to Designate

LGS designations are made through:
1. A Neighbourhood Development Plan — the most common route
2. A Local Plan — where the LPA takes the lead

The community must:
- Identify candidate sites
- Gather evidence to demonstrate they meet the criteria
- Consult with landowners
- Include the designation in the NDP/Local Plan

Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust can help with ecological evidence to support wildlife-rich LGS designations.

## What Protection Does It Give?

Once designated, LGS are protected from development. Planning applications to develop an LGS will be treated in the same way as applications to develop in the Green Belt — only permitted in very special or exceptional circumstances.

This makes LGS one of the strongest tools available to communities to permanently protect local green spaces, parks, orchards, meadows, and wildlife sites.
