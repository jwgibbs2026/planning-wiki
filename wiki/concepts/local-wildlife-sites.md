# Local Wildlife Sites

Local Wildlife Sites (LWS) — also called County Wildlife Sites in some areas — are non-statutory designations identifying the most important wildlife sites outside the national SSSI network. In Gloucestershire, these are managed and recorded by the Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records.

## What Makes a Local Wildlife Site?

LWS are identified using locally agreed criteria based on ecological value. They may include:
- Ancient woodlands below the SSSI threshold
- Traditional orchards
- Unimproved grasslands
- Wetlands, ponds, and watercourses
- Brownfield sites with notable wildlife value
- Calcareous grasslands

LWS are not as legally protected as [[SSSI|SSSIs]], but they are recognised in planning policy through the [[NPPF]] and Local Plans.

## Planning Protection

The [[NPPF]] states that plans and decisions should protect and recover Local Wildlife Sites "from impacts that would significantly harm the features for which they are of recognised importance." This means that damaging a LWS requires strong justification and mitigation.

Local Plans typically include policies protecting LWS and requiring surveys where development may affect them.

## Finding LWS

Local Wildlife Sites are mapped in each district's LPA planning portal and through the Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records. You can request LWS data from GCER to support planning responses or NDP evidence bases.

## Selection Criteria (National PPG)

The national Planning Practice Guidance sets criteria for designating LWS:
- **Size**: Larger sites support viable populations; smaller sites valuable where greenspace is limited
- **Diversity**: Reflects local wildlife and habitat character
- **Naturalness**: Supports natural features and processes
- **Rarity**: Locally exceptional habitats or species
- **Fragility**: Sensitive to climate change or human pressure
- **Connectivity**: Contributes to functional ecological networks
- **Educational value**: Opportunities for learning and engagement

## LPA Duties

LPAs have a statutory duty to:
- Identify and **map** LWSs in development plans
- Include **protective policies** against harm or loss
- **Enhance** sites and connections to wider ecological networks
- Establish **partnerships** with landowners and representative bodies

## Link to LNRS

LPAs must "have regard to" the Local Nature Recovery Strategy when making planning decisions. Local Wildlife Sites commonly coincide with LNRS priority opportunity areas, and BNG off-site units should target these locations where possible.

## The Difference from SSSIs

| Feature | SSSI | Local Wildlife Site |
|---------|------|---------------------|
| Legal status | Statutory — legal protection | Non-statutory — policy protection only |
| Designated by | Natural England | Local partnership (GCER in Gloucestershire) |
| Damage | Criminal offence | Material consideration in planning |
| Number in Gloucestershire | ~350 | ~1,800+ |
