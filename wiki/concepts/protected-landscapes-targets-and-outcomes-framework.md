# Protected Landscapes Targets and Outcomes Framework (PLTOF)

The Protected Landscapes Targets and Outcomes Framework (PLTOF) is a national monitoring and accountability framework for England's protected landscapes — National Parks and Areas of Outstanding Natural Beauty (AONBs/National Landscapes). It was developed by Defra and had its first data release in 2024.

---

## Purpose

PLTOF sets out national targets for protected landscapes and provides a consistent set of indicators to measure progress. It is used to monitor delivery of the CNL Management Plan and to demonstrate compliance with the [[Seek to Further Duty]].

---

## Key targets relevant to the Cotswolds

| Target | Description | CNL Baseline (2024) | 2030 Target | 2050 Target |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|-------------|-------------|
| Target 1 | Restore/create >250,000 ha wildlife-rich habitats within protected landscapes outside protected sites by 2042 (50% of national 500,000 ha target) | 48,000 Ha (23%) | — | 82,000 Ha (40%) |
| Target 2 | 80% SSSIs in favourable condition by 2042 | 49% | — | 80% |
| Target 3 | 60% SSSIs with "actions on track" by 31 Jan 2028 | 14.4% | 60% | — |
| Target 4 | Continuing favourable management of all existing priority habitat outside SSSIs | — | — | — |
| Target 5 | 65–80% of land managers adopt nature-friendly farming on 10–15% of land by 2030 | — | 65–80% | — |
| Target 6 | Net zero by 2050 relative to 1990 | 1,090.32 kt CO2e (2022) | — | Net zero |
| Target 8 | Increase tree canopy/woodland cover by 3% of total land area by 2050 | 35,903 Ha (17.62%) | +1,873 Ha | +9,367 Ha |
| Target 10 | Reduce heritage assets at risk | 62 (0.52%) | 52 | 7 |

---

## Significance for protected landscapes

Protected landscapes cover only 25% of England but are expected to deliver **50% of the national 500,000 ha habitat restoration target** (PLTOF Target 1 = 250,000 ha within protected landscapes by 2042). This means protected landscapes are expected to contribute three times the per-unit-area effort of non-protected land.

## CNL baseline indicators (2024)

Key PLTOF baseline figures for the [[Cotswolds National Landscape]]:
- Wildlife-rich habitat: 41,471 Ha (CNRP 2021 figure); 48,000 Ha (CNL Management Plan figure)
- Ancient woodland: 9,353.1 Ha
- Hedgerows: 13,100 km
- SSSI favourable condition: 49% (above NL average 41.3% and England average 39.5%)
- SSSI actions on track: 14.4%
- Carbon stored: 42,783,399 tonnes
- Agri-environment scheme area: 64,500 Ha (£14,001,800)
- Peat under restoration: 0 Ha
- Light pollution affected: 26.9%
- Tranquillity (most tranquil): 5.9%
- Heritage assets at risk: 62 (0.52%)

---

## Relationship to 30by30

The 30by30 commitment (to protect 30% of land and sea for nature by 2030) relies heavily on protected landscapes. Defra criteria (October 2024) confirm that protected landscapes provide the backbone to 30by30 in England, and that protected landscapes must go further than the national average in delivering nature recovery.

---

## Gloucestershire context

The PLTOF framework is directly relevant to planning and nature management across the Cotswolds parts of Gloucestershire. The [[Cotswolds National Landscape Board]] uses PLTOF indicators alongside local indicators in its 'State of the Cotswolds Report'. Many PLTOF indicators are still in development (nature-friendly farming, agri-environment condition, land cover, agricultural businesses).
