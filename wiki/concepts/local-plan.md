# Local Plan

A Local Plan is a statutory planning document produced by a Local Planning Authority (LPA). It sets out the spatial strategy for an area — where development will go, what land will be protected, and what standards developments must meet.

## What It Contains

A Local Plan typically includes:
- **Spatial strategy** — broad direction for where development will happen
- **Housing allocations** — sites identified for new homes
- **Employment and retail** — sites for commercial development
- **Environmental policies** — protections for [[SSSI|SSSIs]], Local Wildlife Sites, landscapes, green space
- **Biodiversity policies** — often including Biodiversity Net Gain requirements above the national 10% minimum
- **Green Infrastructure** policies
- **Development management policies** — the policies used to assess individual planning applications

## The Plan-Making Process

| Stage | What Happens | Opportunity to Engage |
|-------|-------------|----------------------|
| Evidence gathering | LPA collects data, commissions studies | Feed in wildlife data via Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records |
| Issues and Options consultation | Early-stage public consultation | Submit views on what nature policies should include |
| Preferred Options / Draft Plan | Draft policies published | Respond formally to consultation |
| Pre-submission publication | 6-week statutory consultation | Make formal "representations" — these go to the Inspector |
| Examination | Independent Inspector assesses soundness | Submit written representations; attend hearings |
| Adoption | LPA adopts the final plan | — |

## Getting Nature Policies into Local Plans

Strong Local Plan policies are vital because they apply to every planning application in the area. Examples of nature-friendly policies to push for:
- BNG above 10% (e.g. 20%) for major developments
- Protection of ecological networks beyond designated sites
- Mandatory Green Infrastructure provision
- Requiring Building with Nature standards
- Protecting and enhancing Local Wildlife Sites
- Strong tree protection and new tree planting requirements

See Annex A — NDP Policy Ideas for additional policy suggestions that also apply at Local Plan level.

## Gloucestershire Local Plans

Each of the six LPAs in Gloucestershire has (or is preparing) its own Local Plan. Cheltenham, Gloucester and Tewkesbury Councils are preparing a joint Strategic and Local Plan (the JCS successor).
