# Parish Council

Parish and town councils are the most local tier of local government in England. They operate below district and borough councils and serve individual settlements or communities.

## What Parish Councils Do

Parish councils can:
- Produce Neighbourhood Development Plans (NDPs)
- Feed into Local Plan consultations
- Comment on individual planning applications
- Write Biodiversity Action Plans
- Create community groups and coordinate local nature action
- Apply for grants to fund local projects
- Receive a share of Community Infrastructure Levy receipts once an NDP is in place

## The Biodiversity Duty

Under the Environment Act 2021 and the earlier Natural Environment and Rural Communities Act 2006, parish councils have a statutory duty to have regard to biodiversity in exercising their functions. This includes:
- Considering what they can do to conserve and enhance biodiversity
- Agreeing policies and objectives based on those considerations
- Acting to deliver those policies

## Getting Your Parish Active on Nature

- Check if your parish has an NDP — and if so, whether it has strong nature policies
- Encourage the parish council to write a Biodiversity Action Plan
- Volunteer to sit on a working group or local nature group
- Report wildlife sightings to the Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records
- Get in touch with Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust for support
