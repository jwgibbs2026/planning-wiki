# Biodiversity

Biodiversity is the variety and variability of life on Earth — from the diversity of genes within a species, to the diversity of species within an ecosystem, to the diversity of ecosystems across a landscape.

## Why Does It Matter?

Biodiversity underpins the natural systems that sustain all life, including human civilisation. Healthy, biodiverse ecosystems:
- Purify water and air
- Regulate climate and rainfall
- Pollinate crops and wildflowers
- Cycle nutrients through soils
- Provide food, materials, and medicines
- Support people's physical and mental health

## The Biodiversity Crisis

Globally, biodiversity is declining faster than at any point in human history. In England, the 2019 State of Nature report found that 41% of species assessed had declined in abundance since 1970.

Key drivers of biodiversity loss include:
- Habitat loss and fragmentation
- Agricultural intensification
- Climate change
- Pollution
- Invasive non-native species

## Gloucestershire Context

Gloucestershire was once rich in biodiversity — but like much of England, has seen severe losses over the past 70 years. The county has lost over 97% of its traditional flood meadows, significant areas of ancient woodland, and large populations of once-common farmland birds and insects.

Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust, the Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records, and partner organisations are working to reverse these trends through conservation management, community engagement, and advocacy in the planning system.

## Biodiversity and Planning

The planning system is one of the most significant drivers of biodiversity loss in England — and one of the most important levers for protecting and restoring it. Key tools include:
- Biodiversity Net Gain — mandatory for new developments
- Biodiversity Duty — on public bodies
- Nature Recovery Network — the strategic framework for restoration
- Local Nature Recovery Strategy — spatial planning for nature recovery
- Protected Species and [[SSSI]] protections — legal backstops
- Green Infrastructure — integrating nature into the built environment
