# Neighbourhood Development Plan

A Neighbourhood Development Plan (NDP) is a community-produced planning document that sets out policies for a defined local area. NDPs are produced by parish or town councils and, once "made" (adopted), they carry legal weight in planning decisions.

## Background

NDPs were introduced by the Localism Act 2011. They give communities a formal say in shaping development in their area — something that did not exist before.

## What an NDP Can Do

An NDP can:
- Protect important local green spaces as Local Green Spaces
- Set design standards for new development
- Require wildlife-friendly features in new buildings
- Set policies requiring higher Biodiversity Net Gain than the national minimum
- Protect hedgerows, trees, and ecological networks
- Support the delivery of Green Infrastructure across the community

An NDP **cannot** block all development — it must be in general conformity with the Local Plan and [[NPPF]], and it cannot prevent development that is allocated in the Local Plan.

## The Process

1. **Designation** — the parish council applies to the LPA to designate the neighbourhood area
2. **Preparation** — the community develops the plan, including evidence gathering and community consultation
3. **Pre-submission consultation** — at least 6 weeks public consultation on the draft plan
4. **Submission** — submitted to the Local Planning Authority
5. **Independent examination** — an examiner checks the plan meets "basic conditions"
6. **Referendum** — the local community votes on the plan (requires simple majority)
7. **"Making"** — once passed in referendum, the LPA adopts (makes) the plan

## Basic Conditions

An NDP must meet the "basic conditions":
- Consistent with national planning policy ([[NPPF]])
- In general conformity with the Local Plan
- Contributes to sustainable development
- Compatible with EU obligations (retained in domestic law)

## Funding

Parish councils can apply for up to £10,000 in government grant funding to support NDP preparation. Additional funding may be available from other sources.

Once an NDP is made, the parish council receives a larger share of Community Infrastructure Levy receipts from developments in the area.
