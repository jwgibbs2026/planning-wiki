# Sandford Principle

The Sandford Principle is the rule that when the two statutory purposes of a National Park or AONB/National Landscape conflict, **conservation takes priority over public enjoyment**.

---

## Origin and legal basis

The principle takes its name from Lord Sandford, who chaired a review of National Park policies in 1974. It was given statutory force by **Section 62 of the Environment Act 1995** for National Parks and by the **CROW Act 2000** for AONBs.

The two statutory purposes of the [[Cotswolds National Landscape]] (and all AONBs) are:
1. Conserve and enhance natural beauty
2. Increase public understanding and enjoyment of special qualities

When these purposes conflict — for example, where increased visitor access would damage the landscape or wildlife — the Sandford Principle requires that **conservation takes priority**.

---

## Practical application

The Sandford Principle applies in planning decisions, access management, and recreation policy. In the [[Cotswolds National Landscape]], it is explicitly applied in:
- **Policy UE2 (Access and recreation)** — conservation takes priority over enjoyment when purposes conflict
- **Policy CE13 (Development and infrastructure)** — the highest level of protection applies
- **Policy CE14 (Major development)** — the exceptional circumstances test reflects the primacy of conservation

The principle does not mean that public enjoyment is unimportant — it means that where a genuine conflict arises, conservation wins. In practice, most decisions can be designed to serve both purposes simultaneously.

---

## Relationship to the "seek to further" duty

The [[Seek to Further Duty]] (in force since 26 December 2023) strengthens the conservation purpose further by requiring relevant authorities to **actively further** conservation and enhancement, not merely avoid harm. The Sandford Principle and the "seek to further" duty work together to give conservation the highest priority in the planning system for protected landscapes.

---

## Gloucestershire context

The Sandford Principle applies across the [[Cotswolds National Landscape]], which covers significant parts of Gloucestershire. It is relevant to planning decisions made by Cotswolds District Council, Stroud District Council, Tewkesbury Borough Council, Cheltenham Borough Council, and [[Gloucestershire County Council]] in their respective parts of the CNL.
