# Annex A — NDP Policy Ideas to Support Nature

The following policy ideas are drawn from the GWT Neighbourhood Nature Planning Toolkit. They are suitable for inclusion in Neighbourhood Development Plans and, at a broader scale, Local Plans.

---

## Nature Recovery

> *"All developments should contribute to nature's recovery in Gloucestershire by protecting and strengthening the Nature Recovery Network, which forms part of the Local Nature Recovery Strategy."*

This policy ties development directly to the spatial priorities in the LNRS and Nature Recovery Network.

---

## Biodiversity Net Gain

> *"All developments must deliver ambitious biodiversity net gain, above the 10% statutory minimum."*

The national minimum Biodiversity Net Gain is 10%. NDPs and Local Plans can require higher percentages where there is evidence to justify it. Some areas are pushing for 20% or more.

---

## Green Infrastructure

> *"All major developments should deliver high quality Green Infrastructure, preferably achieving the Building with Nature standards or a similar framework."*

Referencing Building with Nature standards sets a benchmark for quality, not just quantity.

---

## Designated Sites

> *"Developments should ensure protection of internationally, nationally and locally designated sites, including Local Wildlife Sites."*

This covers SSSIs (national), SACs/SPAs (international), and locally-designated Local Wildlife Sites.

---

## SuDS and Flood Risk

> *"All developments should include a Sustainable Urban Drainage (SuDS) scheme, and high-quality natural flood protection, reducing flood risk and creating wildlife habitats."*

[[SuDS]] deliver dual benefits: flood management and habitat creation.

---

## Monitoring

> *"Professional monitoring of biodiversity enhancements must be carried out to demonstrate that they have been achieved, and should be a condition of all planning applications."*

Without monitoring, commitments to deliver Biodiversity Net Gain are unverifiable. This policy ensures accountability.

---

## How to Use These Policies

These are suggested starting points. Policies need to be:
- Written clearly and precisely
- Supported by evidence (habitat surveys, wildlife records from Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records, etc.)
- Consistent with the Local Plan and [[NPPF]]
- Tested through the NDP examination process

Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust can help with tailoring these policies for specific areas.
