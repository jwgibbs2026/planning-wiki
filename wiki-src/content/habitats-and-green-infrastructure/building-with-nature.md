# Building with Nature

Building with Nature (BwN) is the UK's first set of standards for creating high-quality green infrastructure. It was initially developed through a partnership between Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust and the University of the West of England (UWE Bristol).

## What It Is

Building with Nature provides a framework of **12 standards** that developers, designers, and planners can use to ensure they are delivering green infrastructure that genuinely benefits nature and people, throughout every stage of the development process.

The standards cover:
- **Wellbeing** — connecting people with nature in their everyday lives
- **Ecology** — supporting and enhancing local biodiversity
- **Water** — managing water sustainably
- **Movement** — creating connected, active, nature-rich routes

## The Accreditation Awards

Building with Nature offers an independent accreditation scheme — the **BwN Accredited Awards** — which recognises planning policies, planning applications, and built developments that meet the standards. There are three levels:
1. **Foundation** — meets the basic standards
2. **Accredited** — meets all standards to a good level
3. **Accredited with Excellence** — exceptional performance

Achieving accreditation provides independent verification that a development's GI commitments are credible.

## Why It Matters for Planning

Local Plans and NDPs can reference Building with Nature standards as the benchmark for high-quality Green Infrastructure. The GWT toolkit suggests requiring all major developments to achieve BwN standards or equivalent.

Although BwN is no longer managed by GWT, it is still recommended as the standard for sustainable development GI in Gloucestershire and beyond.
