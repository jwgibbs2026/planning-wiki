# National Standards for Sustainable Drainage Systems (SuDS)

These national standards set the rules for how new development must handle rainwater. The idea behind them is to drain water the way nature does — slowing it down and treating it at or near the surface, close to where it falls — rather than rushing it underground through pipes. This page explains the standards in plain terms; the main [[SuDS]] page gives the wider context.

The [[Local Planning Authority]] approves the drainage design, and the [[Lead Local Flood Authorities|Lead Local Flood Authority]] supplies flood-risk and drainage information. The standards apply to new development that needs planning permission in England (trunk roads and retrofits aside), and a scheme has to show it meets them from the earliest design stage.

## The seven standards, in plain terms

1. **Where the water goes.** Rainwater must drain by the best available route in a set order: reuse it first, then let it soak into the ground, then to a watercourse, then a surface-water sewer, and only as a last resort a combined sewer. Higher cost alone isn't a good enough reason to drop down the list.
2. **Everyday rain.** The system must soak up the first 5 mm of rain without any runoff — handling most of it in summer and around half in winter.
3. **Extreme rain.** Discharge must be held down to roughly greenfield (undeveloped) rates so the development doesn't make flooding worse downstream, and the design must allow for climate change.
4. **Water quality.** The drainage must clean the water as it passes through, with extra treatment for higher-risk sites.
5. **Amenity.** The features should also improve the place — greenery that cools streets and space for walking, play, and recreation where it fits.
6. **Biodiversity.** SuDS should create living, varied habitat that adds to nature. Those habitats can count toward mandatory [[Biodiversity Net Gain]] and should support the [[Local Nature Recovery Strategy]].
7. **Looking after it.** There must be a clear plan for maintaining the system for the life of the development.

## How it fits together

Done well, SuDS is designed in from the very start, woven into the site's levels, roads, [[Green Infrastructure|green infrastructure]], and planting — a connected "management train" of features that slows water, cleans it, and adds habitat all at once. It also brings in the [[Lead Local Flood Authorities|Lead Local Flood Authority]] as a key body to engage early, and links closely to the [[Environment Act 2021]] and the [[Building with Nature]] standard.
