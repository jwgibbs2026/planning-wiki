## What this wiki covers

The Gloucestershire Planning & Nature Wiki is a knowledge base connecting planning law, policy, biodiversity, and local nature in Gloucestershire. It is designed to be navigated visually through the Obsidian graph view, with plain-English pages that link to related topics. The wiki serves individuals, community groups, parish councillors, ecologists, and developers who want to understand and engage with the planning system.

## Key themes and connections

The wiki covers eleven major knowledge domains in depth:

**Planning system and community engagement** — from national policy (NPPF, Environment Act) through to local plans, neighbourhood development plans, and practical guidance for communities responding to applications. The GWT Neighbourhood Nature Planning Toolkit provides the foundation for this strand.

**Sustainable drainage (SuDS)** — anchored by the national statutory standards from MHCLG/Defra, this strand establishes SuDS as mandatory for all new developments requiring planning permission in England. The seven standards create a comprehensive framework: Standard 1 (runoff destination hierarchy with cost-cannot-justify principle), Standard 2 (5mm interception with seasonal targets), Standard 3 (greenfield runoff rate benchmarks and climate change allowances), Standard 4 (water quality management trains), Standard 5 (amenity and placemaking), Standard 6 (biodiversity, BNG integration, and LNRS alignment), and Standard 7 (maintenance and structural integrity). LPAs act as SuDS Approving Bodies; Lead Local Flood Authorities provide flood risk context. In Gloucestershire, Gloucestershire County Council is the LLFA.

**Protected species in planning — bats, GCN, otters, badgers, water voles, wild birds, hazel dormice** — seven detailed strands covering the full procedural chain for each species group, anchored by Natural England's standing advice documents. Key structural distinctions are maintained: EPS (Habitats Regs), wild birds (WCA 1981, no development licence), badgers (Protection of Badgers Act 1992), and water voles (WCA 1981, non-EPS).

**Ancient woodland and trees in planning** — anchored by the joint Natural England and Forestry Commission standing advice, covering the strongest habitat protection category in the English planning system. Ancient woodland and ancient trees are irreplaceable; compensation alone cannot justify approval.

**Protected landscapes** — anchored by the Cotswolds National Landscape Management Plan 2025–2030, covering the Seek to Further Duty, Sandford Principle, major development tests, 20% BNG requirement, and the PLTOF monitoring framework.

**Green infrastructure** — SuDS management trains are now established as one of the most concrete GI delivery mechanisms in new development. The strand connects SuDS, Building with Nature, dark corridors, green bridges, and the Natural England GI Framework.

**Biodiversity Net Gain** — the wiki now covers multiple delivery mechanisms for BNG: on-site habitat creation, SuDS habitats, and off-site credits/habitat banks. The integration of SuDS Standard 6 with BNG calculations is an important practical point for developers and communities.
