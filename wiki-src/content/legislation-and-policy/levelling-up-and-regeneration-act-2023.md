# Levelling Up and Regeneration Act 2023

The Levelling Up and Regeneration Act 2023 (LURA) introduced reforms to the planning system aimed at accelerating housebuilding, improving plan-making, and giving communities more power over design standards.

## Key Planning Provisions

### Faster Build-Out
The Act gives LPAs new powers to hold housing developers to account over the speed at which they build out permitted developments. LPAs can now decline further applications from developers with poor delivery records.

### National Development Management Policies
The Act introduces "National Development Management Policies" (NDMPs) — a new layer of national policy that will sit alongside [[NPPF]] and automatically take precedence over local plan policies on certain topics.

### Infrastructure Levy
The Act contains provisions for a new Infrastructure Levy to replace the existing Community Infrastructure Levy (Community Infrastructure Levy) and Section 106 agreements in the long term. The timeline for this transition is uncertain.

### Street Votes
Allows residents on a street to hold a vote on a proposed development design, giving communities a new tool to shape the built environment.

### Plan-Making Reform
Introduced a new, faster process for Local Plan preparation, with a target of 30 months from start to adoption (compared to often 5–10 years under the old system).

## Nature Implications

The Act itself does not weaken existing biodiversity protections, but the faster plan-making process means communities and organisations like Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust have shorter windows to engage with and influence Local Plan development.
