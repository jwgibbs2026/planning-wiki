# Planning and Infrastructure Act 2025

The Planning and Infrastructure Act 2025 is a significant piece of legislation making wide-ranging reforms to England's planning system, including the introduction of the Nature Restoration Fund and changes to infrastructure delivery.

## Key Provisions for Nature

### Nature Restoration Fund (NRF)

The most significant nature-related provision. Allows developers to pay into a central fund — administered by Natural England through **Environmental Delivery Plans (EDPs)** — instead of undertaking site-specific environmental mitigation.

- EDPs replace site-by-site habitat assessments and species licences for covered impacts
- First EDPs expected 2026, targeting nutrient neutrality in priority catchments
- Future expansion to cover other protected species and habitats

See Nature Restoration Fund for full detail.

### Planning Reform

The Act also includes wider planning system reforms aimed at accelerating housing delivery, simplifying the plan-making process, and improving infrastructure funding mechanisms. The specific provisions are separate from the nature-focused NRF.

## Relationship to Earlier Legislation

- Builds on Environment Act 2021: strengthens strategic nature delivery
- Sits alongside Levelling Up and Regeneration Act 2023: further simplification of developer contributions
- NRF designed to complement (not replace) Biodiversity Net Gain: BNG still applies; NRF covers HRA and species licence obligations separately
