# Environment Act 2021

The Environment Act 2021 is the UK's most significant piece of environmental legislation in decades. It replaced EU environmental law with a domestic framework following Brexit and introduced binding targets for nature's recovery.

## Statutory Targets

The Act sets legally binding targets in four priority areas:
- **Biodiversity** — halt the decline in species abundance by 2030; improve biodiversity by 2042
- **Air quality** — reduce fine particulate matter (PM2.5) concentrations
- **Water** — reduce water company pollution incidents and improve river water quality
- **Waste** — increase resource productivity and reduce residual waste

## Biodiversity Net Gain (BNG)

The Act introduced the mandatory requirement for all new developments in England to deliver a minimum **10% Biodiversity Net Gain**. This is one of the most significant new tools for protecting and enhancing nature through the planning system.

## The Biodiversity Duty

The Act strengthened the existing Biodiversity Duty, requiring public bodies (including Local Planning Authorities) to:
- Consider what they can do to conserve and enhance biodiversity
- Agree policies and specific objectives based on those considerations
- Act to deliver those policies and objectives
- Report on what they have done (publication duty)

Failure to act on the biodiversity duty is a failure of legal compliance.

## Local Nature Recovery Strategies (LNRS)

The Act requires responsible authorities (typically county councils) to produce Local Nature Recovery Strategies — spatial plans identifying where nature recovery action is most needed. In Gloucestershire, Gloucestershire County Council is the responsible authority.

## Office for Environmental Protection (OEP)

The Act established the Office for Environmental Protection — an independent watchdog to hold government and public bodies to account for meeting environmental laws and commitments. Members of the public can make complaints to the OEP if they believe environmental law is not being followed.

## Nature Recovery Network

The Act supports the Nature Recovery Network — a joined-up system of protected and improved habitats that will help England reach the 30x30 target (protecting 30% of land for nature by 2030).
