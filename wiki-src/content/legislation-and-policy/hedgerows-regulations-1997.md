# Hedgerows Regulations 1997

The Hedgerows Regulations 1997 protect important rural hedgerows from removal. They are administered by local planning authorities.

## How the Regulations Work

Before removing a hedgerow (other than a garden hedge), a landowner or developer must notify the local planning authority (LPA). The LPA then assesses whether the hedgerow is "important" using criteria set out in the regulations:

- **Ecological** — does it support a diverse range of wildlife or Protected Species?
- **Historical** — is it more than 30 years old and associated with a historic feature?
- **Archaeological** — does it mark a pre-1850 boundary or contain archaeological features?
- **Landscape** — is it in or adjacent to a protected landscape?

## Protections in Practice

If the LPA determines a hedgerow is "important", it can issue a **Hedgerow Retention Notice**, preventing removal for at least two years. Removing an important hedgerow without permission, or in breach of a Retention Notice, is a criminal offence.

## Relevance to Planning

When commenting on a Planning Application that involves hedgerow removal, you should:
1. Check whether any hedgerows on the site may meet the "important" criteria
2. Request that the LPA requires a Hedgerow Survey from the developer
3. Reference the Hedgerows Regulations in your response

Hedgerows are vital wildlife corridors — they connect habitats, support Protected Species (bats, dormice, hedgehogs), and are part of the wider Green Infrastructure network.
