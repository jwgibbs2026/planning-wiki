# Natural Environment and Rural Communities Act 2006

The Natural Environment and Rural Communities Act 2006 (NERC Act) was a landmark piece of legislation that reshaped how the government and public bodies approach nature conservation.

## Key Provisions

### Creation of Natural England
The Act enabled the creation of Natural England as an independent government agency (merging English Nature, the Countryside Agency, and parts of the Rural Development Service). Natural England became operational in October 2006 and is responsible for protecting England's nature and landscapes.

### Biodiversity Duty — Section 40
Section 40 of the NERC Act introduced the original "biodiversity duty" on public bodies in England. It requires:
> *"Every public authority must, in exercising its functions, have regard, so far as is consistent with the proper exercise of those functions, to the purpose of conserving biodiversity."*

This duty was significantly strengthened by the Environment Act 2021, which added requirements to publish plans and report progress.

### Section 41 Species and Habitats of Principal Importance
Section 41 required the Secretary of State to publish a list of habitats and species of principal importance for conserving biodiversity in England (the "S41 list"). This list is used to guide conservation action and is relevant when assessing planning applications — developments affecting S41 habitats or species must demonstrate how they will be protected or compensated.

### SSSI Duties
The Act gave local government a statutory duty to further the conservation and enhancement of [[SSSI|SSSIs]] in both operational activities and decision-making.

## Relevance Today

Although some provisions have been updated by the Environment Act 2021, the NERC Act remains the foundational statute for Natural England's existence and the basis of the biodiversity duty in its original form.
