# 30x30 Commitment

The 30x30 commitment is the UK government's pledge to protect **30% of land and 30% of seas** for nature by 2030. It is one of the most significant nature policy commitments of recent decades and underpins much of the legislative and policy framework in this wiki.

## Origin

The 30x30 target emerged from the **Kunming-Montreal Global Biodiversity Framework**, agreed at COP15 in December 2022 in Montreal. 196 countries — including the UK — signed the agreement, committing to protecting 30% of their land and ocean areas as an effective measure against the global biodiversity crisis.

The UK was actually one of the first countries to adopt the 30x30 target domestically, pledging it in 2020 ahead of the international agreement.

## What "Protected" Means

The UK counts land and sea toward 30x30 using a definition of "effectively protected and managed" areas. This includes:
- SSSIs in favourable or recovering condition
- SACs and SPAs (the international sites)
- National Parks and National Landscapes (AONBs)
- Local Nature Reserves
- Land managed under long-term conservation agreements (including [[Conservation Covenants]])
- Some agri-environment scheme land meeting the criteria

As of 2024, approximately **8% of England's land** meets the threshold — a very significant gap to reach 30% by 2030.

## Connection to English Legislation and Policy

The 30x30 commitment directly connects to:
- **Environment Act 2021**: established Biodiversity Net Gain, the Biodiversity Duty, and [[Local Nature Recovery Strategy|Local Nature Recovery Strategies]] — all tools to drive land into better condition for nature
- **Local Nature Recovery Strategy**: the spatial tool for identifying where nature recovery should happen to contribute to 30x30
- **Biodiversity Net Gain**: BNG ensures development does not reduce the 30x30 baseline; off-site BNG habitats can contribute to the target
- **Agri-environment schemes** ([[agri-environment-schemes|SFI, Countryside Stewardship]]): the primary mechanism for bringing agricultural land into effective management for nature at scale
- **Landscape Recovery**: large-scale land use change to create and restore habitats contributing to 30x30

## GWT's 30% Target

Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust has adopted the 30x30 framework locally, setting a target of **30% of all land in Gloucestershire** managed for nature's recovery by 2030 — approximately 30,000 additional hectares. GWT aims to deliver 10,000 of those hectares directly through work with farmers and landowners.

This directly aligns the Gloucestershire LNRS priorities with the national 30x30 commitment.

## Why It Matters for Planning

30x30 provides the overarching policy context for:
- Why Biodiversity Net Gain is a minimum rather than a ceiling — 10% BNG is not sufficient to reach 30x30 alone
- Why the [[Cotswolds National Landscape]] has a 20% BNG requirement — protected landscapes must go further
- Why off-site BNG should be directed to LNRS priority areas — maximising the contribution toward 30x30
- Why the Nature Restoration Fund takes a strategic approach — individual site mitigation alone won't achieve 30x30
