# SSSI — Site of Special Scientific Interest

A Site of Special Scientific Interest (SSSI) is a legally protected area notified by Natural England because of its special wildlife or geological interest. SSSIs are the most important wildlife sites in England.

## How SSSIs Are Protected

SSSIs are notified under the Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981 (as amended by the CROW Act 2000). The protections mean:
- The landowner/occupier cannot carry out "operations likely to damage the SSSI" without Natural England's consent
- LPAs must notify Natural England when planning applications could affect a SSSI
- Natural England can object to and delay planning decisions that would damage a SSSI

Damaging a SSSI without consent is a criminal offence.

## Condition of SSSIs

Natural England monitors the condition of SSSIs and classifies each unit as:
- **Favourable** — meeting its conservation objectives
- **Unfavourable recovering** — not yet in good condition but trending in the right direction
- **Unfavourable no change** or **Unfavourable declining** — in poor condition and not improving

Many SSSIs in England (including in Gloucestershire) are not in favourable condition due to air pollution, agricultural practices, water quality, and development pressures.

## SSSIs in Gloucestershire

Gloucestershire has a rich diversity of SSSIs, including:
- Meadows in the Severn and Avon flood plains
- Ancient woodlands in the Forest of Dean and Cotswolds
- Grasslands in the Cotswold Hills
- Wetlands along the Severn Estuary

Records of wildlife within and around SSSIs can be found through the Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records.

## International Designations

Many of the best SSSIs are also given international designations:
- **Special Area of Conservation (SAC)** — under the Habitats Directive (retained in domestic law via Conservation of Habitats and Species Regulations 2017)
- **Special Protection Area (SPA)** — for internationally important bird populations
- **Ramsar site** — for internationally important wetlands

These international designations trigger **Habitats Regulations Assessment** requirements for any plans or projects that might affect them.
