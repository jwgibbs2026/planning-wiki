# Natural England — Great Crested Newts Planning Guidance

When a development could affect great crested newts (GCN), [[Natural England]]'s standing advice tells the local planning authority what to take into account. This page explains it plainly; the main [[Great Crested Newts in Planning]] page has the full detail.

## How great crested newts are protected

GCN are a [[European Protected Species]] under the Habitats Regulations and a [[Species of Principal Importance]]. As with bats, it is an offence to harm them or to damage the ponds and land they depend on, and most works need a licence.

## The numbers worth remembering

A few specific thresholds drive GCN work in practice:

- **500 metres** — any suitable pond or ditch within 500 m of the site triggers a survey.
- **Four seasons** — survey data older than four survey seasons is too old to rely on. See [[GCN Surveys]].
- **Two ponds** — if a breeding pond is lost, at least two good replacement ponds must be created and established **before** the original is destroyed.

## Two ways to license the work

- The traditional route is a site-by-site [[EPS Mitigation Licence]], which uses the standard three-part test.
- The newer alternative is [[District Level Licensing]]: instead of mitigating on their own site, the developer pays into a scheme that creates and maintains newt habitat where it will do most good across the district. This is often faster and delivers better outcomes for the newts.

Either way, the authority should confirm a licence is likely before granting permission.
