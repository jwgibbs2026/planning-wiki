# Natural England — Hazel Dormice Planning Guidance

When a development could affect hazel dormice, [[Natural England]]'s standing advice tells the local planning authority what to take into account. This page explains it plainly; the main [[Hazel Dormice in Planning]] page has the full detail.

## How hazel dormice are protected

Hazel dormice are a [[European Protected Species]] and a [[Species of Principal Importance]], and they are classed as Vulnerable on England's Red List for mammals. As with bats and newts, harming them or damaging their habitat is an offence, and most works need a licence.

## When a survey is needed

A survey is expected where records suggest dormice could be present, or simply where the site has the woodland, hedgerow, or scrub they live in — the habitat alone is enough to trigger one. Surveys range from searching for nibbled hazelnuts and nests on small jobs to nest tubes, boxes, and footprint tunnels on larger ones (see [[Dormouse Surveys]]). A survey up to three years old is usually fine if the habitat hasn't changed.

Two rules shape how a survey is used:

- **Assume presence everywhere.** If dormice turn up anywhere on the site, you must treat all suitable habitat on it as occupied.

## What you should do, in order

- **Avoid** — redesign and re-time the work to keep the habitat they use.
- **Improve and connect** — manage woodland (thinning, coppicing, planting), add nest boxes, and use [[Green Bridges]] to reconnect habitat split by a road or other barrier.
- **Translocate** — only as a last resort, with a specialist, a suitable receptor site, and a licence.

Most works need an [[EPS Mitigation Licence]], and the authority should line up planning conditions with what the licence requires.
