# Dormouse Surveys

Dormouse surveys are required when development proposals affect woodland, hedgerow, or scrub habitat, or when historical records suggest hazel dormice may be present. Surveys must be carried out by a competent ecologist meeting CIEEM standards. Handling live dormice requires a survey licence from Natural England.

---

## When Surveys Are Required

See [[Hazel Dormice in Planning]] for full trigger criteria. In summary, surveys are required when:

- Historical records or distribution data (from [[NBN Atlas]] or Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records) indicate dormice presence near the site
- The development affects woodland, hedgerow, or scrub — even in the absence of confirmed records

The precautionary principle applies: suitable habitat is sufficient to trigger survey.

---

## Survey Methods

### Visual Nest and Hazelnut Searches

The simplest survey method, appropriate for minor projects or initial scoping assessments. Ecologists search for:

- **Woven nests**: Dormouse nests are distinctive — tightly woven balls of grass, honeysuckle bark, and leaves, typically found in dense shrub layer vegetation
- **Gnawed hazelnuts**: Dormice leave a characteristic smooth, angled tooth mark around the hole in a hazelnut shell, distinguishable from the marks left by wood mice or squirrels

This method is non-invasive and does not require a survey licence, but it is less sensitive than tube-based methods and may miss low-density populations.

### Nest Tubes

Artificial cardboard or plastic tubes are placed along hedgerows, woodland edges, and scrub at regular intervals (typically every 10–20 metres). Tubes are checked periodically through the active season (April–October) for signs of dormouse use: nests, hair, or droppings.

Nest tubes are the standard method for significant projects. They require multiple visits across the active season to achieve adequate detection probability.

### Nest Boxes

Wooden nest boxes designed for dormice (similar to bird boxes but with the entrance hole facing the tree trunk) are fixed to trees or shrubs. They are checked periodically for occupancy. Boxes can also serve a dual purpose as mitigation features once surveys are complete.

Checking occupied boxes requires a survey licence from Natural England as dormice may be handled.

### Footprint Tracking Tunnels

Tunnels baited with a food attractant and lined with tracking paper or an ink pad are placed along likely dormouse routes. Footprints left on the paper can confirm presence without the need to handle animals. This method is useful as a non-invasive complement to tube surveys.

## Survey Currency

Surveys up to **three years old** are acceptable if habitats on the site remain unchanged since the survey was conducted. If habitats have changed materially, new surveys are required regardless of the age of existing data.

This three-year rule is specific to dormice. It differs from the four-season data currency rule applied to great crested newts — survey currency rules are species-specific.

---

## Seasonal Timing

Hazel dormice hibernate from approximately **October to April**. Surveys must be conducted during the active season (April–October). Key considerations:

- Nest tubes and boxes should be in place for at least one full active season before a definitive absence conclusion can be drawn
- Early season surveys (April–May) may detect emergence from hibernation
- Late season surveys (September–October) may detect pre-hibernation activity and juveniles from summer breeding
- Multiple visits across the season are required for robust presence/absence conclusions

Construction timing should account for hibernation: clearance works affecting dormouse habitat should ideally be scheduled outside the active season (i.e., October–April), though this must be balanced against other ecological constraints (e.g., nesting bird season, March–August).

---

## Surveyor Competency

Surveys should be conducted by ecologists with demonstrable competence in dormouse survey methods, in line with CIEEM professional standards. Handling live dormice (including checking occupied nest boxes or tubes) requires a **survey licence** from Natural England.

---

## Presence Assumption Rule

If dormice are detected anywhere on a site, their presence must be assumed throughout all suitable habitat on the site. This means:

- Survey scope cannot be limited to the immediate development footprint if suitable habitat extends beyond it
- A single positive detection triggers site-wide mitigation obligations
