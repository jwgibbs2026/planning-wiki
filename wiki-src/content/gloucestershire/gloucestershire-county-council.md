# Gloucestershire County Council

Gloucestershire County Council is the upper-tier local authority for Gloucestershire. It is not a Local Planning Authority for most planning purposes (district councils hold LPA functions), but it has significant roles in strategic planning, transport, minerals and waste planning, and nature recovery.

---

## Role in the Cotswolds National Landscape

[[Gloucestershire County Council]] covers **63.7% of the [[Cotswolds National Landscape]]** area — the largest share of any single local authority. This makes it a key partner for the [[Cotswolds National Landscape Board]] in delivering the CNL Management Plan 2025–2030.

From April 2024, Local Enterprise Partnership (LEP) roles and responsibilities were transferred to Gloucestershire County Council.

The County Council is a strategic partner of the **Cotswolds Plus Local Visitor Economy Partnership (LVEP)**, which was accredited by VisitEngland in 2023 and covers Cotswolds Tourism, Forest of Dean and Wye Valley Tourism, Marketing Cheltenham, Visit Gloucester, and Visit Gloucestershire.

---

## Planning functions

As a county council, Gloucestershire County Council is responsible for:
- **Minerals and waste planning** — the Gloucestershire Minerals Local Plan and Waste Local Plan
- **Highways** — as highways authority, it is a relevant authority subject to the [[Seek to Further Duty]] for decisions affecting the CNL
- **Education and social care** — relevant to developer contributions and infrastructure planning

The six district councils (Cotswolds District Council, Stroud District Council, Tewkesbury Borough Council, Cheltenham Borough Council, Gloucester City Council, Forest of Dean District Council) are the Local Planning Authorities for most planning decisions.

---

## Local Nature Recovery Strategy

Gloucestershire County Council is the responsible authority for preparing the **Gloucestershire Local Nature Recovery Strategy (LNRS)** — one of six LNRSs coincident with the [[Cotswolds National Landscape]]. The LNRS is expected to be compatible with the CNL Management Plan and the [[Cotswolds Nature Recovery Plan]].
