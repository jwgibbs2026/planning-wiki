# Wye Valley Woodlands SAC

The Wye Valley Woodlands Special Area of Conservation (SAC) is an internationally designated series of ancient semi-natural woodlands on the steep slopes of the lower Wye gorge, straddling the England–Wales border in west Gloucestershire and Monmouthshire. It is designated for its mixed ravine woodland (Tilio-Acerion), yew woodland, and old sessile oak woods, and for its important populations of [[Bats in Planning|bats]] — including lesser and greater horseshoe bats — and [[Hazel Dormice in Planning|hazel dormice]].

As a SAC it is protected under the [[Conservation of Habitats and Species Regulations 2010]], and development affecting the woodlands or the bat populations that forage and commute along the valley can trigger a [[Habitats Regulations Assessment]]. The woodlands are a core part of the ecological interest of the [[Wye Valley National Landscape]].

## Gloucestershire Context

The Wye Valley Woodlands SAC lies in the Forest of Dean district and the [[Wye Valley National Landscape]], a key consideration for development around the lower Wye and the western edge of the county. Its horseshoe bat interest makes lighting and connectivity ([[Dark Corridors]]) central to nearby development. It is listed in [[Gloucestershire Internationally Designated Sites]].
