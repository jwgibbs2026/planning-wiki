# Gloucestershire Internationally Designated Sites

Gloucestershire contains or overlaps with a significant number of internationally designated wildlife sites. These carry the highest level of nature protection in the planning system, triggering [[habitats-regulations-assessment|Habitats Regulations Assessment]] for any plan or project that may significantly affect them.

## Special Areas of Conservation (SACs)

SACs are designated under the Conservation of Habitats and Species Regulations 2017 to protect the most important habitats and species in Europe.

| SAC | Location | Key qualifying habitats/species |
|-----|----------|-------------------------------|
| **Severn Estuary** | Southern/western Gloucestershire border | Estuaries, mudflats, Atlantic salt meadows, Sabellaria reefs, river lamprey, twaite shad |
| **Wye Valley Woodlands** | West Gloucestershire / Forest of Dean | Beech forests, lime forests of slopes/screes/ravines, yew woods |
| **Cotswold Beechwoods** | East Gloucestershire / Cotswolds | Most westerly extensive beech forests in UK; floristically rich |
| **Rodborough Common** | Stroud district | Species-rich dry calcareous grassland; important invertebrate assemblages |
| **Dixton Wood** | Near Tewkesbury border | Violet click beetle (*Limoniscus violaceus*); ancient wood pasture |
| **Bredon Hill** | Tewkesbury borough (north) | Lowland calcareous grassland; orchid-rich |

> Note: verify the complete list against the MAGIC dataset (magic.defra.gov.uk) — additional smaller SACs may exist within Gloucestershire.

## Special Protection Areas (SPAs)

SPAs are designated to protect populations of rare, vulnerable, or migratory bird species.

| SPA | Location | Key qualifying bird species |
|-----|----------|----------------------------|
| **Severn Estuary** | Southern/western border | Dunlin, redshank, curlew, shelduck, wigeon, Bewick's swan, white-fronted goose |
| **Walmore Common** | Near Westbury-on-Severn | Overwintering Bewick's swans (significant proportion of UK population) |

## Ramsar Sites

Ramsar sites are internationally important wetlands designated under the Ramsar Convention.

| Ramsar site | Location | Key features |
|-------------|----------|-------------|
| **Severn Estuary** | Southern/western border | Internationally important intertidal wetland; unique tidal range; migratory waterbirds |

## Planning Implications

### Habitats Regulations Assessment Triggers
Any development or plan that *may* significantly affect any of these sites — alone or in combination with others — requires a [[habitats-regulations-assessment|Habitats Regulations Assessment]]. This includes:
- Development within the catchment that could increase nutrients or sediment
- Development near woodland SACs that could affect hydrology, air quality, or species connectivity
- Development on or near limestone grassland SACs (Rodborough, Cotswold Beechwoods, Bredon Hill)
- Any development affecting migratory bird flight lines or roosting areas near SPAs

### Nutrient Neutrality
The **Severn Estuary** SAC and Ramsar are the primary drivers of [[nutrient-neutrality|nutrient neutrality]] requirements across Gloucestershire's river catchments. Development draining to the Severn or Avon may require nutrient mitigation.

### In-Combination Effects
The HRA "in combination" test means that even small developments must be screened if, combined with other recent or planned development, their cumulative effect could significantly affect a designated site.

## National Nature Reserves (NNRs) in Gloucestershire

NNRs are nationally important sites managed primarily for nature conservation.

| NNR | Location | Key habitats |
|-----|----------|-------------|
| **Cotswold Commons and Beechwoods** | Central Cotswolds | 7.5 km² — largest nature reserve in the Cotswolds; limestone beechwood |
| **Highbury Wood** | Wye Valley | Ancient broadleaved woodland on the Wye gorge |

## Where to Find Designated Site Data

- **MAGIC** (magic.defra.gov.uk): authoritative national dataset for all designated sites in England
- **GCER**: local records including Local Wildlife Sites alongside national designations
- **Natural England**: consult on all applications affecting SSSIs, SACs, and SPAs
