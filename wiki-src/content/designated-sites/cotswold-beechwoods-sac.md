# Cotswold Beechwoods SAC

The Cotswold Beechwoods Special Area of Conservation (SAC) is an internationally designated site on the Cotswold escarpment south-east of Cheltenham, centred on the beech woodlands and limestone grassland around Cranham, Buckholt, and the Cotswold Beechwoods National Nature Reserve. It is designated for its Asperulo-Fagetum beech forests on calcareous soils and its populations of [[Bats in Planning|bats]] (including greater and lesser horseshoe bats).

As a SAC it is protected under the [[Conservation of Habitats and Species Regulations 2010]], so development with the potential to affect it — including development some distance away that affects bat foraging and commuting routes, air quality, or recreational pressure — can trigger a [[Habitats Regulations Assessment]]. The site is one of the key drivers of the precautionary bat-survey and [[Dark Corridors|dark-corridor]] requirements applied to development on the escarpment.

## Gloucestershire Context

The Cotswold Beechwoods SAC sits within the [[Cotswolds National Landscape]] and is a recurring consideration for development around Cheltenham, Stroud, and the escarpment villages. Its horseshoe bat populations made it central to the environmental assessment of the [[Green Bridges|A417 Missing Link]] scheme. It is listed in [[Gloucestershire Internationally Designated Sites]].
