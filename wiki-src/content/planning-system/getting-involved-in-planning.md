# Getting Involved in Planning

There are multiple points in the planning system where individuals, community groups, and parish councils can have their say — and make a genuine difference for nature.

## At the Parish Level

- Check whether your parish has a Neighbourhood Development Plan (NDP). If so, does it include strong nature policies? If not, now is the time to push for them.
- Talk to your parish council about their NDP — either to improve an existing one or to start one.
- Encourage your parish council to write a Biodiversity Action Plan to set clear targets for nature locally.
- Feed wildlife data into the Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records to build the evidence base.

## At the District Level

- Read your district council's Local Plan environment and biodiversity chapters.
- Respond to consultations when the Local Plan is being reviewed or updated — this is when policies can be strengthened.
- Attend planning committee meetings when ecologically sensitive applications are being decided.

## On Individual Planning Applications

See Tips for Responding to Planning Applications for detailed guidance.

Key points:
- Comment during the statutory consultation window
- Base comments on [[NPPF]], Local Plan, and Neighbourhood Development Plan policies
- Reference any known protected species or habitats on or near the site
- Report any wildlife sightings to the Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records before or during the application process

## For Large Infrastructure Projects

If a major new road, energy project, or similar scheme is proposed in your area, it may be a Nationally Significant Infrastructure Project decided by the Planning Inspectorate. You can submit representations directly to the Inspectorate's examination process.

## Collaboration

Reach out to Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust for support with NDPs, Local Plan consultations, or ecological evidence. GWT can help communities build the case for nature in planning.
