# Planning System Overview

The planning system oversees the use and development of all land in the UK. It ensures that development happens in the right place, at the right time, for the benefit of communities, the economy, and the environment.

## What Does It Do?

The system identifies where development is needed and which areas should be protected — for communities, for wildlife, and for future generations. Key decisions are made by local planning authorities, [[Parish Council|Councillors]], and planning officers.

## The National Level

At national level, the **Secretary of State for Housing, Communities and Local Government** sets overall policy direction through the [[NPPF]]. The Planning Inspectorate handles planning appeals, examinations of Local Plans, and Nationally Significant Infrastructure Projects.

## The Local Level

Local planning is led by district and borough councils acting as Local Planning Authorities (LPAs). They produce Local Plans and make decisions on planning applications.

Below them, parish and town councils produce Neighbourhood Development Plans (NDPs), which give communities a direct say in shaping their area.

## Key Plans in the System

| Plan | Who Writes It | What It Does |
|------|--------------|--------------|
| [[NPPF]] | Central Government | Sets national planning policy |
| Local Plan | District/Borough Council | Sets local development goals and protections |
| Neighbourhood Development Plan | Parish/Town Council | Sets planning policies at community level |

## Nature in the Planning System

The planning system is one of the most important levers for protecting wildlife. Policies in the [[NPPF]], Local Plans, and NDPs can require developers to protect habitats, deliver Biodiversity Net Gain, and provide quality Green Infrastructure.

Key legislation underpinning nature in planning includes the Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981, the Conservation of Habitats and Species Regulations 2017, and the Environment Act 2021.
