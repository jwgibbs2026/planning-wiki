# Planning Hierarchy

The planning system in England operates across several tiers, from national government down to local communities.

## The Tiers

```
National Government
  └─ Secretary of State for Housing, Communities & Local Government
       └─ Sets national policy (NPPF)
       └─ Decides Nationally Significant Infrastructure Projects

Planning Inspectorate
  └─ Examines Local Plans
  └─ Handles planning appeals
  └─ Recommends decisions on NSIPs

County Council
  └─ Highways, minerals, waste planning
  └─ Not typically the LPA for general development

District / Borough / Unitary Authority  ← Most planning decisions happen here
  └─ Acts as the Local Planning Authority (LPA)
  └─ Produces the Local Plan
  └─ Decides planning applications

Parish / Town Council
  └─ Produces Neighbourhood Development Plans
  └─ Consulted on planning applications
  └─ Can feed into Local Plan consultations
```

## Gloucestershire Authorities

In Gloucestershire, the six district/borough councils each act as LPAs:
- Cheltenham Borough Council
- Gloucester City Council
- Stroud District Council
- Forest of Dean District Council
- Tewkesbury Borough Council
- Cotswolds District Council

Gloucestershire County Council handles strategic matters (highways, minerals, waste) but not general planning applications.

## How the Tiers Interact

Plans at each level must be consistent with the tier above:
- Local Plans must conform to the [[NPPF]]
- NDPs must conform to the Local Plan and NPPF
- Planning applications are assessed against all applicable plans

This means that getting good nature policies into the NPPF, Local Plans, and NDPs creates a cascading benefit right down to individual decisions.
