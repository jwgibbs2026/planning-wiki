# Tips for Responding to Planning Applications

By the time a planning application is submitted, large-scale design changes are unlikely. But a well-argued response can still secure conditions that protect nature, trigger additional ecological surveys, or influence the planning committee's decision.

## Where to Start

1. Find the application on your district council's planning portal. All six Gloucestershire LPAs have searchable portals.
2. Check the application documents for any ecological reports (Phase 1 habitat surveys, protected species surveys, ecological impact assessments).
3. Check whether the proposed site or its surroundings are covered by any designations: [[SSSI]], Local Wildlife Sites, or local ecological networks.

## What to Include in Your Response

**Policy hooks** — frame your response around relevant policies:
- [[NPPF]] Chapter 15 (Conserving and enhancing the natural environment)
- Your district's Local Plan biodiversity policies
- Your Neighbourhood Development Plan nature policies (if one exists)
- Statutory requirements under the Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981 and Conservation of Habitats and Species Regulations 2010

**Biodiversity Net Gain** — check that the application demonstrates the mandatory 10% Biodiversity Net Gain required under the Environment Act 2021.

**Ecological surveys** — if you know of Protected Species or important habitats on or near the site, note that proper surveys should be required as a condition.

**Evidence** — attach any relevant wildlife records. You can request records from the Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records to support your case.

## What Not to Focus On

Comments about common species (rabbits, foxes, deer) are unlikely to carry weight. Stick to:
- Protected Species (bats, great crested newts, badgers, breeding birds, etc.)
- Designated sites ([[SSSI]], Local Wildlife Sites)
- Significant habitat loss (ancient woodland, hedgerows covered by the Hedgerows Regulations 1997)

## After You've Commented

- Send a copy of your response to your local district councillor and parish council
- If the application is approved, check that any conditions relating to ecology are being complied with
- Report any ongoing wildlife sightings at the site to the Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records
