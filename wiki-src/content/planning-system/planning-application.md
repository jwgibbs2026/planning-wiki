# Planning Application

A planning application is a formal request to a Local Planning Authority (LPA) for permission to carry out development. The LPA will assess the proposal against national and local policies before granting or refusing permission.

## The Process

1. **Pre-application** — Developers are encouraged to engage with the LPA and specialist organisations (like Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust) before submitting. This is the best point to influence outcomes for nature.
2. **Submission** — The application is lodged with the LPA, including any required surveys (ecology, flood risk, heritage, etc.).
3. **Consultation** — The public and statutory consultees are notified. This is your window to comment.
4. **Decision** — The LPA's planning officer (or planning committee for larger schemes) grants, refuses, or grants with conditions.
5. **Appeal** — If refused, the applicant can appeal to the Planning Inspectorate.

## Commenting on an Application

By the time an application is submitted, it is hard to fundamentally change its design. However, comments can:
- Add conditions to protect nature
- Trigger additional ecological surveys
- Prompt referral to the planning committee

When commenting, refer to:
- [[NPPF]] policies on biodiversity and environment
- Local Plan policies for the area
- Neighbourhood Development Plan policies if one exists
- Relevant wildlife legislation (see Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981, Conservation of Habitats and Species Regulations 2010)

> Focus on protected species and designated habitats rather than common species. Comments about protected species carry more legal weight.

## Ecology Surveys

Developers must carry out ecological surveys to support their applications, but LPAs don't always know when to require them. If you know of wildlife on or near a proposed site, report sightings to the Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records as evidence.

## Biodiversity Requirements

All developments in England are now required to deliver a minimum **10% Biodiversity Net Gain** under the Environment Act 2021. Larger developments may be required to achieve more, particularly where Local Plan policies set higher standards.
