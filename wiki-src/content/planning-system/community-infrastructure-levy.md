# Community Infrastructure Levy

The Community Infrastructure Levy (CIL) is a charge that local planning authorities can require developers to pay towards the cost of infrastructure needed to support new development.

## How It Works

The LPA sets a CIL charging schedule (in £ per square metre of new development). When a CIL-liable development is granted permission, the developer must pay the levy before work begins.

The funds are then used by the LPA and local communities to pay for infrastructure such as:
- Roads and transport improvements
- Schools and education facilities
- Green spaces and parks
- Sports facilities
- Nature and habitat enhancement

## The Neighbourhood Portion

Where a Neighbourhood Development Plan is in place, **25% of CIL receipts** from development in that neighbourhood area are passed directly to the parish or town council. Without a made NDP, the neighbourhood proportion is only 15%.

This is a significant financial incentive for parishes to produce an NDP. The funds can be used for local projects, including wildlife projects, habitat enhancement, and community green space improvements.

## Section 106 Agreements

Alongside CIL, larger or more complex developments may also be subject to **Section 106 (S106) planning obligations** — legal agreements between the developer and the LPA that secure specific infrastructure, affordable housing, or ecological mitigation measures. CIL cannot be used for site-specific mitigation; that remains the role of S106.

## Exemptions and Reliefs

Several categories are exempt or eligible for reduced CIL:
- **Social housing**: full relief
- **Charitable development**: 100% relief where wholly/mainly charitable
- **Minor development**: under 100 sq m net floor area (single dwellings of any size are always liable)
- **Residential extensions/annexes**: owner-occupiers may qualify — must apply before work begins
- **Self-build**: full exemption if criteria met before commencement

**Important**: Most exemptions require a commencement notice before work starts. Failure to submit incurs a surcharge of 20% of the notional charge (capped at £2,500).

## Annual Infrastructure Funding Statements

From December 2020, LPAs must publish annual Infrastructure Funding Statements showing how CIL and S106 funds will be allocated. These are publicly available documents showing infrastructure priorities and developer contribution spending.

## The Future

The Levelling Up and Regeneration Act 2023 includes provisions for a new "Infrastructure Levy" to eventually replace CIL and S106, though the transition timeline is uncertain.
