# Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust

Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust (GWT) is the county's leading nature conservation charity. GWT's mission is to preserve, enhance and connect wildlife within Gloucestershire.

## What GWT Does

- Manages nature reserves across Gloucestershire
- Engages with planning to protect and enhance nature
- Supports communities and parish councils in developing NDPs and Biodiversity Action Plans
- Runs wildlife recording schemes and supports the Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records
- Works with developers and LPAs to improve outcomes for nature
- Co-created Building with Nature (with UWE Bristol)
- Campaigns on planning policy including Local Plan consultations

## GWT and Planning

GWT engages with developers at the design stage — before applications are submitted — for larger schemes. This pre-application engagement is often more effective than commenting after submission.

GWT has a team of ecologists, planners, and community engagement staff who can:
- Review draft NDPs and suggest nature policies
- Advise on Biodiversity Net Gain and Green Infrastructure requirements
- Provide training and guidance for community groups
- Support the development of Biodiversity Action Plans

## The Neighbourhood Nature Project

This wiki is based on the GWT **Neighbourhood Nature Planning Toolkit** — a resource created to help communities understand and engage with the planning system to support nature locally.

## Contact and Resources

GWT's website contains a range of resources including wildlife gardening guides, volunteer opportunities, and information about planning for nature. Contact GWT directly for support with NDPs, BAPs, or local planning issues.
