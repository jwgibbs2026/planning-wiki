# CIEEM — Chartered Institute of Ecology and Environmental Management

CIEEM is the professional membership body for ecologists and environmental managers in the UK and Ireland. It sets the competency standards, codes of professional conduct, and good-practice guidance that govern how ecological work is carried out in the planning system.

CIEEM is the reference point for whether an ecologist is suitably qualified to undertake protected-species surveys and assessments. Its most influential publication for planning is the **Guidelines for Ecological Impact Assessment (EcIA)**, the standard methodology behind [[Ecological Impact Assessment]]. Local planning authorities and [[Natural England]] expect surveys — such as [[Bat Surveys]], [[Dormouse Surveys]], and [[Water Vole Surveys]] — to be carried out by appropriately experienced, often CIEEM-qualified, ecologists, and CIEEM membership grades (e.g. Chartered Ecologist) are commonly cited as evidence of competence.

## Gloucestershire Context

When a Gloucestershire LPA requires an ecological survey to validate a [[Planning Application]], it will expect the work to follow CIEEM standards and be undertaken by a competent ecologist. CIEEM guidance underpins the survey effort, timing, and reporting standards applied to [[Protected Species]] across the county.
