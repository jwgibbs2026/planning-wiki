# Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records

The **Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records** (GCER) is the county-level Local Environmental Records Centre (LERC) for Gloucestershire. It collects, manages, and provides access to biodiversity and environmental data for the county, serving local planning authorities (LPAs), developers, ecologists, conservation organisations, and the public.

---

## Role in Planning

GCER is the primary source of local species and habitat data for planning decisions in Gloucestershire. Its data is used to:

- Identify whether protected species have been recorded near a proposed development site
- Inform ecological surveys and impact assessments, including Preliminary Ecological Appraisals (PEAs)
- Support Biodiversity Net Gain baseline assessments
- Provide evidence for Local Nature Recovery Strategy mapping

Developers, ecologists, and LPAs should consult GCER when assessing whether protected species surveys are required for a development site. For [[European Protected Species]] — including bats, great crested newts, and otters — GCER data is typically the first local check before commissioning surveys.

GCER data should be used **alongside** [[NBN Atlas]] (the national database) — GCER often holds more recent, localised, or detailed records than the national dataset.

---

## Data Coverage

GCER holds records for a wide range of species and habitats, including:

| Species / Dataset | Planning Relevance |
|---|---|
| Bats | Roost records, commuting routes; EPS survey trigger assessment; see [[Bats in Planning]] |
| Great crested newts | 500m pond survey trigger; pond and population records; see [[Great Crested Newts in Planning]] |
| Otters | River corridor records; water proximity survey trigger; see [[Otters in Planning]] |
| Badgers | Sett records (note: confidentiality considerations apply); habitat suitability data; see [[Badgers in Planning]] |
| Water voles | Catchment distribution; riparian survey trigger assessment; see [[Water Voles in Planning]] |
| Wild birds | Breeding and wintering bird records; see [[Wild Birds in Planning]] |
| Hazel dormice | Distribution records for woodland and hedgerow habitats; see [[Hazel Dormice in Planning]] |
| Ancient woodland | Ancient Woodland Inventory data and veteran tree locations; see [[Ancient Woodland in Planning]] |
| Local Wildlife Sites | Boundary data |
| Priority habitats | Habitat mapping |
| Historical ecological surveys | Background baseline data |

> **Note on badger data:** Badger survey results are kept confidential to prevent persecution — GCER may hold aggregated rather than precise sett location data.

## How to Access GCER Data

1. Submit a data request via the GCER website, specifying the site location and search radius. Data searches are provided for a fee.
2. Data is typically provided as a species list with grid references and dates, covering the defined search radius around the development site.
3. Use the data to assess whether historical records trigger survey requirements.
4. Remember: **absence of a GCER record does not mean absence of a species** — if suitable habitat exists, surveys are required regardless of whether a record exists.

---

## Relationship to NBN Atlas

GCER and the [[NBN Atlas]] are complementary resources:

- **GCER** — county-level, more complete for Gloucestershire, may hold records not yet aggregated nationally, requires a data request for some datasets
- **NBN Atlas** — national coverage, publicly accessible and free to search, may not include all locally held records

Best practice for any development in Gloucestershire is to consult both sources. Natural England's standing advice for otters and water voles specifically recommends checking the NBN Atlas; GCER provides the local detail that the national database may lack.

---

## Gloucestershire Context

GCER operates across all six Gloucestershire districts and works closely with the Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust and the county's local planning authorities. It is particularly valuable for:

- Identifying dormouse records in hedgerow-rich landscapes of the Cotswolds and Forest of Dean fringe
- Water vole distribution in Severn tributaries and Cotswold Water Park
- Bat roost records in the Severn Vale, Cotswold escarpment, and urban and rural areas
- Great crested newt pond records across the county
- Otter presence along the Severn and its tributaries
- Badger sett data (aggregated; see note above)
- Confirming ancient woodland boundaries and veteran tree locations

Parish councils and community groups can also contact GCER to obtain species data for their area when preparing Neighbourhood Development Plans or responding to planning applications.
