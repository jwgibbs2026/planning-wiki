# Environment Agency

The Environment Agency (EA) is the executive non-departmental public body, sponsored by [[Defra]], responsible for protecting and improving the environment in England — with a particular remit over water, flooding, and pollution. In the planning system the EA is a statutory consultee on development affecting flood risk, water quality, and watercourses.

The EA's planning roles include advising local planning authorities on [[Flood Risk Assessment|flood risk assessments]] and the Sequential and Exception Tests, regulating discharges to controlled waters, managing main rivers and flood defences, and providing the evidence base (flood zones, water body status) that underpins [[Flooding and Natural Flood Management]] and [[SuDS]] requirements. It also works alongside [[Natural England]] on [[Nutrient Neutrality]] and the protection of designated water-dependent sites such as the [[Severn Estuary]].

## Gloucestershire Context

The EA is central to development in the [[Flood Risk Assessment|flood-prone]] Severn Vale and around Tewkesbury, where the confluence of the Severn and Avon creates significant flood risk. It manages flood defences along the Severn and is a key consultee for any development near the [[Severn Estuary]] and the county's main rivers. EA water-quality data informs nutrient-neutrality and [[Water Voles in Planning|water vole]] assessments across Gloucestershire's catchments.
