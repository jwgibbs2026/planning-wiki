# GCER — Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records

This page explains what GCER does and why it matters in planning. The main [[Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records]] page gives the wider context.

GCER is Gloucestershire's local environmental records centre, run as part of the [[Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust]]. It holds more than two million biological and environmental records for the county and supplies that data to planners, ecologists, and communities. Its records also underpin the evidence base for the [[Local Nature Recovery Strategy]].

## What it provides

GCER carries out data searches, GIS mapping and analysis, and nature recovery mapping, and it offers GIS training. In short, it can tell you what's already known about wildlife on or near a particular site.

## Why it matters in planning

A GCER data search is routinely needed to support an ecological assessment for a planning application, the environmental assessment behind a Local Plan, a [[Habitats Regulations Assessment]], or the evidence base for a neighbourhood plan. It confirms whether protected species, [[Local Wildlife Sites]], or priority habitats are present on or near a site — usually the first thing to check at the pre-application stage. It is also the local counterpart to the national [[NBN Atlas]].
