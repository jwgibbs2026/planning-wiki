# Historic England

Historic England is the government's statutory adviser on the historic environment in England. It is a statutory consultee in the planning system for development affecting designated heritage assets — listed buildings, scheduled monuments, registered parks and gardens, and conservation areas.

While its primary focus is heritage rather than nature, Historic England's remit frequently overlaps with biodiversity in the planning system: historic farm buildings and barns often support [[Bats in Planning|bats]] and [[Barn Owls in Planning|barn owls]] (see [[Barn Conversions and Wildlife]]); traditional orchards, veteran trees, and historic parkland are both heritage and ecological assets; and the setting of heritage assets can shape [[Green Infrastructure]] and landscape design. Heritage and ecology are weighed together as material considerations under the [[NPPF]].

## Gloucestershire Context

Gloucestershire's market towns, Cotswold villages, and historic estates mean heritage constraints apply to a large share of development — particularly conversions and extensions in the [[Cotswolds National Landscape]]. Many of these same buildings trigger protected-species surveys, so Historic England guidance and ecological survey requirements often run in parallel on the same application.
