# Cotswolds National Landscape Board

The Cotswolds National Landscape Board (CNL Board) is the statutory body responsible for managing the [[Cotswolds National Landscape]]. Its legal name is the **Cotswolds Conservation Board**, established by Parliamentary Order in 2004. It is one of only two Conservation Boards in England — the other being the Chilterns Conservation Board.

**Address:** The Threshing Barn, Marsden Manor Farm, Rendcomb, Cirencester, Gloucestershire GL7 7EU
**Website:** www.cotswoldsaonb.org.uk
**Chair (as of February 2025):** Brendan McCarthy

---

## Statutory purposes

The Board has two statutory purposes under the CROW Act 2000:
1. Conserve and enhance the natural beauty of the CNL
2. Increase public understanding and enjoyment of its special qualities

It also has a duty to foster the economic and social wellbeing of people living in the CNL. The [[Sandford Principle]] applies: when the two purposes conflict, conservation takes priority.

---

## Membership

The Board has 37 members:
- 15 nominated by principal local authorities
- 8 nominated by parish councils
- 14 appointed by government

## What the Board does and does not do

The Board does **not** own or directly manage any land in the CNL. Its role is to:
- Produce and review the statutory Management Plan
- Provide planning advice to local planning authorities
- Commission research and evidence
- Coordinate partnerships across 15 local authorities
- Support delivery through the Cotswold Voluntary Wardens

The **Cotswold Voluntary Wardens** support the Board's work: 497 active volunteers contributed 52,971 hours in 2023/24, improving 496 public rights of way features and clearing/improving 78,593 metres of path.

---

## Monitoring and delivery

The Board monitors progress through the [[Protected Landscapes Targets and Outcomes Framework]] and local indicators, reported in the 'State of the Cotswolds Report'. It uses a three-yearly Business Plan Framework and annual work programme.

In 2023/24, 56% of planning decisions were made in line with CNL Board recommendations. Only 10% of planning decisions in 2024 explicitly referred to the [[Seek to Further Duty]]; only 6% adequately addressed it.

---

## Gloucestershire context

The Board's area overlaps significantly with Gloucestershire. [[Gloucestershire County Council]] covers 63.7% of the CNL; Cotswolds District Council covers 43.8%. The Board works with all Gloucestershire local planning authorities and is a statutory consultee for planning applications affecting the CNL.
