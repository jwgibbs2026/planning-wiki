## Overview

GWT's strategy to 2030, "Bringing Nature Back", sets out GWT's ambition to stop and reverse wildlife declines in Gloucestershire by 2030. GWT is the largest charity in the county dedicated to nature's recovery, managing over 1,000 hectares of nature reserves and working with farmers and landowners across the county. It has 28,000 members and hundreds of regular volunteers.

The strategy is framed around the climate and ecological emergency and the need to act "at scale and at pace."

---

## Vision and Purpose

**Vision**: A natural world in recovery, where nature-based solutions are tackling the climate crisis, and everyone is inspired to take action for wildlife.

**Purpose**: To ensure nature's recovery together with the people of Gloucestershire.

---

## The Problem

- The UK is one of the most nature-depleted countries in the world
- Gloucestershire has lost 70% of traditional orchards
- Less than 1.5% of wildflower-rich meadows remain (down from 40% in the Cotswolds since the 1930s)
- If declines cannot be halted before 2030, losses will be irreversible

---

## Three Strategic Goals

### 1. Stopping and Reversing Wildlife Declines

**Targets by 2030:**
- Grow land owned or managed by GWT from 1,100 to **1,700 hectares**
- Deliver **30% of all land in Gloucestershire** managed for nature (~30,000 additional hectares); GWT to contribute 10,000 hectares working with farmers and landowners
- Create or improve **300 hectares** of urban/accessible wildlife-rich green space, including a new Nature Park in Gloucester (benefitting 90,000 people)
- Create **10 Nature Recovery Zones** around the county, based on GWT nature reserves and extending across the wider landscape
- Create **300 hectares of new wetlands** ("Wildbelt") along the River Severn — the "Severn wetscape network"
- Explore reintroduction of beavers for natural flood management

### 2. More People Taking Action for Nature

**Targets by 2030:**
- Reach **1 in 4 people in Gloucestershire**
- Connect with **25,000 school children** and 30,000 more young people
- Mobilise **15,000 people** and support **100 communities** to take meaningful action for wildlife
- Expand education offer and digital engagement (WilderGlos platform)

### 3. Normalising Natural Solutions to Climate Change

**Targets by 2030:**
- Lead **Severn Treescapes**: 2,000 hectares of enhanced tree cover; 3 million new trees connecting Forest of Dean/Wye Valley with Wyre Forest; 600,000 people with access; 1,700 tonnes of carbon captured
- Support **regenerative agriculture**: create or enhance 5,000 hectares of farmland for wildlife and climate
- Establish a wetscape network in the Severn Vale with natural processes in 30% of river catchments
- Increase conservation grazing management by over 60%

---

## Enabling Priorities

1. Grow and diversify funding
2. Value and invest in staff and volunteers (target: 650 active volunteers)
3. Steward assets and measure impact
4. Lead and develop ecological evidence base — through GCER and the Nature Recovery Network
5. Live GWT's values (including carbon positive by 2030)

---

## Planning Relevance

GWT's strategy underpins its engagement with the planning system:
- **Building with Nature** — GWT's standard for new development putting nature at its heart
- **Nature Recovery Zones** — used as a spatial framework for advising on planning applications
- **GCER** — GWT leads GCER as Gloucestershire's Local Environmental Records Centre, providing the biodiversity evidence base for planning decisions
- **30x30 ambition** — GWT's 30% target aligns with the government's 30x30 commitment under the Environment Act 2021
