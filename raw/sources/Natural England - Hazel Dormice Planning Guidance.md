# Hazel Dormice: Advice for Making Planning Decisions

**Source**: Natural England standing advice (gov.uk)
**URL**: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/hazel-dormice-advice-for-making-planning-decisions
**Type**: Protected species standing advice — material planning consideration

---

## Overview

This Natural England guidance assists local planning authorities (LPAs) in evaluating development applications affecting hazel dormice, a European protected species in England.

## Legal Protection Status

Hazel dormice receive protection under the Conservation of Habitats and Species Regulations 2017 and the Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981. Key prohibited activities include:

- Deliberately kill, injure, disturb or capture them
- Damaging breeding sites and resting places
- Possessing or transporting them (dead or alive)
- Selling or offering them for sale

The species is classified as vulnerable on England's Red List for Mammals and holds principal importance status under the Natural Environment and Rural Communities Act 2006.

## Survey Requirements

Request ecological surveys when:
- Historical records or distribution data suggest dormice presence
- Development affects woodland, hedgerow, or scrub habitat

Survey methods may include:
- Visual nest and hazelnut searches (minor projects)
- Nest tubes, boxes, or footprint tracking tunnels (significant impacts)

Surveys should be current or from the previous active season; surveys up to three years old are acceptable if habitats remain unchanged. If dormice are found in one area, assume presence throughout suitable habitats on the site.

## Development Impact Assessment

Developers must demonstrate how proposals avoid or mitigate harm. Harmful activities include habitat removal, disturbance from noise/light, foraging habitat loss, and hedgerow removal that isolates populations.

## Mitigation and Compensation Measures

Strategies may include:
- Habitat redesign and timing adjustments
- Habitat improvement (thinning, coppicing, planting)
- Nest box installation
- Green bridges connecting fragmented habitats
- Translocation as a last resort

Translocation requires specialist expertise, suitable receptor sites, and licensing approval.

## Licensing and Planning Conditions

Developers may need hazel dormice mitigation licences. LPAs should coordinate conditions with Natural England licensing requirements before granting permission.

## Site Management and Monitoring

Long-term monitoring plans should track population status, vegetation management, foraging/nesting habitat availability, and mitigation effectiveness.
