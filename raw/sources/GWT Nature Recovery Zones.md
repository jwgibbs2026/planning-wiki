## Overview

Nature Recovery Zones are GWT's strategic conservation approach that extends wildlife protection beyond the boundaries of individual nature reserves. GWT works closely with neighbouring landowners, farmers, and land managers to create larger, wildlife-friendly connected landscapes.

The approach implements the framework from the 2010 Lawton Report, which called for sites that are:
- **Bigger**: Larger protected areas
- **Better**: Improved habitat quality
- **More**: Additional wildlife sites created
- **Joined up**: Connected across landscapes

This creates what GWT terms "super reserves" — interconnected habitat networks providing better long-term species protection than isolated reserves.

## Gloucestershire's Nature Recovery Zones

GWT has defined ten Nature Recovery Zones across Gloucestershire:

| Zone | Key Features |
|------|-------------|
| The Cotswolds Escarpment | Limestone ridge; species-rich grasslands; ancient woodlands |
| Severn Hams | Wetlands and floodplains; waterfowl and wading birds |
| Windrush Valley | River valley; floodplain meadows and grasslands |
| Slad Valley | Limestone grassland; orchids; white-clawed crayfish |
| Stroud Commons | Grasslands and woodlands; bat habitats |
| The Golden Valley | Large blue butterflies; diverse insect assemblages |
| Central Forest | Pine marten populations; diverse habitat mosaic |
| Lower Wye and Tidenham Chase | Ancient woodlands; heathlands |
| Leadon Valley | Arable farmland; wildlife-rich fragments; wild daffodils |
| Lower Woods to Westonbirt | Ancient woodlands; species-rich meadows |

## Relationship to Planning and Policy

Nature Recovery Zones form a key component of GWT's vision for Gloucestershire's landscape-scale conservation. They are directly relevant to:

- **Local Nature Recovery Strategy (LNRS)**: GWT's zones inform and align with the Gloucestershire LNRS's identification of strategic nature recovery areas
- **Biodiversity Net Gain (BNG)**: Off-site BNG habitat units can be targeted within Nature Recovery Zones to maximise ecological coherence
- **Green Infrastructure planning**: Zones provide a spatial framework for green infrastructure delivery in Local Plans and Neighbourhood Plans
- **Planning consultations**: GWT uses this framework when providing ecological advice on planning applications affecting priority landscapes

## GWT Planning Services

GWT offers planning consultation services to help developers, LPAs, and communities understand biodiversity impacts and opportunities within and adjacent to Nature Recovery Zones.
