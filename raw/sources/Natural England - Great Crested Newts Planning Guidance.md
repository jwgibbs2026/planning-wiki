# Great Crested Newts: Advice for Making Planning Decisions

**Source**: Natural England standing advice (gov.uk)
**URL**: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/great-crested-newts-advice-for-making-planning-decisions
**Type**: Protected species standing advice — material planning consideration

---

## Overview

Natural England's standing advice for assessing planning applications affecting great crested newts (GCN) in England. This guidance is a material planning consideration for local planning authorities.

## Legal Protection of GCN

GCN are European protected species. It is illegal to:

- Deliberately kill, injure, disturb or capture them
- Deliberately destroy their eggs
- Damage or destroy their breeding sites and resting places
- Possess, transport, sell, or exchange them

GCN are also listed as species of principal importance under the Natural Environment and Rural Communities Act 2006.

## District Level Licensing Schemes

District level licensing (DLL) offers an alternative to traditional mitigation licenses. This approach provides new or better habitats in targeted areas to benefit their wider population and streamlines the process for developers.

## Survey Requirements

Developers must provide surveys if:

- Distribution records suggest GCN may be present
- A suitable water body such as a pond or ditch within 500 metres of the development exists
- Development includes refuges within 500m of aquatic habitats

Survey scope typically covers water bodies within 250m, potentially extending to 500m without dispersal barriers.

**Survey data requirements:**
- No older than 4 survey seasons
- Must reflect current site status
- Should meet industry standards
- May include eDNA sampling, population surveys, and habitat assessments

## Impact Assessment Framework

| Impact Level | Habitat Type |
|---|---|
| High | Breeding ponds; terrestrial habitat within 50m of ponds; habitat fragmentation |
| High to Medium | Non-breeding occupied ponds; terrestrial habitat 50–250m from ponds |
| Medium | Partial habitat destruction; post-development interference |
| Low | Temporary disturbance; habitat beyond 250m |

## Mitigation and Compensation Measures

**Avoidance priorities:**
- Leave ponds in place
- Enhance surrounding habitat
- Provide winter hibernation shelters
- Connect suitable habitats

**Breeding pond compensation requirements:**
- Create minimum 2 new high-quality replacement ponds
- Ensure new ponds are ready for GCN before the old pond is destroyed
- Allow at least one full growing season for establishment
- Safeguard or replace other ponds within 500m

## Licensing and Planning Conditions

Developers must obtain a GCN mitigation licence if activities affect GCN and DLL is unavailable or unused.

Planning authorities should:
- Coordinate conditions with ecologists to avoid conflicts with licensing requirements
- Confirm Natural England will issue a licence before granting permission
- Include conditions for mitigation and compensation strategies

## Site Management and Monitoring

Management plans should address:
- Aquatic vegetation and pond maintenance
- Shading control around margins
- Grassland and woodland management
- Public access restrictions

Monitoring should verify mitigation effectiveness with remedial work as needed.
