# Gloucestershire Centre for Environmental Records (GCER)

**Source**: GCER website
**URL**: https://www.gcer.co.uk/
**Type**: Local environmental records centre — service overview
**Status**: Current; new pricing effective April 2026

---

## Overview

GCER is Gloucestershire's Local Environmental Records Centre (LERC). It functions as a unique, authoritative source of information about the wildlife and natural environment of Gloucestershire. GCER operates as part of the Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust.

---

## Database Holdings

- Over 2 million biological and environmental records for Gloucestershire
- Continually updated through liaison with field recorders and GIS data capture
- Covers species occurrences, habitat data, protected sites, and environmental features

---

## Services

| Service | Description |
|---------|------------|
| Data searches | Searches of the biological database for specific sites or areas |
| GIS analysis and mapping | Spatial analysis and map production |
| Nature recovery mapping | Habitat and biodiversity data for nature recovery planning |
| GIS training | Training programmes for professionals |

---

## Primary Users

GCER supplies data to:
- Conservation professionals and ecological consultants (for ecological reports)
- Commercial utilities
- Local planning authorities (LPAs)
- National agencies (Natural England, Environment Agency)
- Parish councils and community groups (for neighbourhood plans and Parish Nature Recovery Plans)

---

## Role in Planning

GCER provides the primary environmental evidence base for planning decisions in Gloucestershire. LPAs and ecological consultants routinely obtain GCER data searches when assessing planning applications that may affect biodiversity. Data searches are typically required by:
- Ecological assessments supporting planning applications
- Strategic Environmental Assessments (SEA) for Local Plans
- Habitat Regulations Assessments (HRA)
- Neighbourhood Plan ecological evidence bases

GCER data can confirm presence/absence of protected species, local wildlife sites, and habitats of principal importance within or near a development site.

## Access

- Data searches available via the GCER website (www.gcer.co.uk)
- New pricing schedule effective April 2026
- Natural Capital Mapping tools accessible via the Natural Capital Mapping Project
- Option to submit wildlife records to contribute to the database

---

## Relationship to LNRS

GCER's data underpins the Gloucestershire Local Nature Recovery Strategy (LNRS), providing the species and habitat evidence base for identifying priority areas and actions.
