# Badgers: Advice for Making Planning Decisions

**Source**: Natural England standing advice (gov.uk)
**URL**: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/badgers-surveys-and-mitigation-for-development-projects
**Type**: Protected species standing advice — material planning consideration

---

## Overview

Natural England's standing advice for local planning authorities (LPAs) regarding badger protection in planning applications. This guidance applies to England and forms part of the protected species planning advice collection.

## Legal Protection

Badgers and their setts are protected under the Protection of Badgers Act 1992. It is unlawful to:

- Take, injure, ill-treat or kill a badger — or attempt these actions
- Treat a badger cruelly
- Obstruct, disturb, damage or destroy a badger sett or any part of it
- Sell, possess or control a badger (or any part, alive or dead)
- Mark, tag or ring a badger

Developers may require a badger licence for activities affecting badgers.

## Survey Requirements

Request surveys when:
- Historical or distribution records indicate badger activity in the area
- Suitable habitat exists for sett building or foraging

Surveys should document current badger usage, identify sett locations, classify sett types, and estimate territorial boundaries. The National Biodiversity Network Atlas can help assess historical records.

Ecologists must demonstrate competence through CIEEM qualifications. Surveys should remain confidential to prevent badger harm.

## Development Impact Assessment

Assess whether proposals cause:
- Sett damage or loss
- Habitat isolation or foraging area reduction
- Disturbance during occupancy (noise, lights, vibration, chemical use)

## Avoidance and Mitigation Strategies

Developers should prioritise avoiding impacts through:
- Layout design preventing territorial isolation
- Eliminating artificial lighting near setts
- Maintaining vegetation cover
- Minimising noise and vibration
- Keeping machinery away from setts
- Avoiding dusk-to-dawn activity periods

Mitigation measures include maintaining foraging spaces, establishing habitat connectivity via tunnels or green corridors, and creating artificial setts as compensation for losses.

**Note:** Translocation is discouraged due to tuberculosis transmission risks and welfare concerns.

## Planning Conditions and Licensing

Before granting permission, planners must:
- Coordinate conditions with relevant ecologists
- Ensure alignment with badger licence requirements
- Confirm Natural England's licensing capacity

Long-term site management plans should monitor sett integrity and habitat availability post-development.
